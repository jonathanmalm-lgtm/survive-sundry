The team huddle is at 9:00 AM in the lobby. All staff, all volunteers, all 47 people who showed up before 9 AM on a Sunday because they believe in something. You believe this is a waste of time because you could have twenty more minutes to fix the disasters presented in sound check.

The kids ministry leader steps forward and acknowledges what everyone already knows — you're short-staffed this morning. The holes are real. But — and here she pauses for effect — 'God won't give you more than you can handle.' She says this with the confidence of someone citing Scripture. She is citing Scripture. The verse she is thinking of is 1 Corinthians 10:13, which is about resisting temptation, not worship teams missing two vocalists and a bassist.

The campus pastor produces a $5 Chick-fil-A gift card. This was decided at staff meeting — the team quietly nominated whose volunteer was most visibly on the edge. It wasn't a long discussion. There was a consensus. He calls Terry's name. Terry is a parking lot volunteer who has been standing in the cold since 6:30 AM, whose wife just had a baby, who has not slept more than three hours in a week, and who is smiling the smile of a man running entirely on faith and adrenaline. Terry receives the card with the specific expression of someone who didn't know they were being watched but is very glad they were.

Then Greg is introduced. Greg was a worship pastor at a well-known church that recently imploded because their pastor had a moral failure. He is here to 'observe and encourage.' Specifically to observe you. Greg is wearing a very good jacket. Greg has the quiet confidence of someone whose church had a monthly lighting budget larger than what you make in five years. Greg makes eye contact with you and nods like he already knows things about you.

You drank milky iced coffee this morning. You knew it would affect your voice. You drank it anyway.

## A

You seem secure. Greg is actually very kind. But now he's at sound check and watching Cody.

## B

Safe. Professional. Greg mentions to the pastor later that you seemed 'guarded.' This is noted.

## C

Awkward silence. Pastor laughs uncomfortably. Greg says 'no no no.' He is, a little bit.

## D:win

You are already halfway across the lobby before anyone has fully processed what you said. Greg laughs. A real laugh. 'No,' he says. 'The network was fine.' After service he finds you. 'That was an interesting introduction,' he says. 'I've been praying for that church,' you say. 'I know you have,' he says. Greg's assessment of your worship ministry is somehow still positive. This bothers you more than a negative review would have.

## D:lose

You are already halfway across the lobby before anyone has fully processed what you said. The campus pastor finds you at sound check four minutes later. He does not raise his voice. He says three sentences. You remember all three of them for the rest of the day. Greg's assessment of your worship ministry is glowing. This also bothers you. Nothing about this morning is going the way you expected.

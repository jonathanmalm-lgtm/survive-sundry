The team huddle starts in the lobby. It's the chance for everyone to get hyped up, even though you wish you had a few more minutes to get things ready for the service.

The kids ministry leader steps forward and acknowledges what everyone already knows — you're short-staffed this morning. The holes are real. 'But as it says in Scripture...' — and here she pauses for effect — 'God won't give you more than you can handle. So you got this!'

You cringe at how out of context that was. But kids just need Jesus, not exegesis.

The campus pastor produces a $5 Chick-fil-A gift card to give to one lucky volunteer who has been going above and beyond. Really it's just for the volunteer closest to burnout. Something to give them that extra boost to keep going. Today, Terry gets it. His wife just had a baby. Still, he's setting up and volunteering in the parking lot every single Sunday, either trying to escape the new baby or just super committed to the cause. Terry tears up a bit when he receives the gift card. A few waffle fries will go a long way to fix burnout.

Then Pastor introduces Greg – a worship pastor at a well-known church that recently imploded because their pastor had a moral failure. Pastor says Greg is here to 'observe and encourage.' Pastor looks directly at you as he says it. Greg is wearing a very good jacket. Greg has the quiet confidence of someone whose church had a monthly lighting budget larger than what you make in five years. Greg makes eye contact with you and nods like he already knows things about you.

You drank milky iced coffee this morning. You knew it would affect your voice. You drank it anyway. Now Greg gets to hear your phlegmy voice.

A little bit of warning would have been nice.

## A

Look at you, being all secure. Greg is actually very kind. It seems like he genuinely just wants to help. Plus you both bond over the kids ministry leader's horrible misuse of that Scripture.

## B

Ooh. Not good. Pastor hunts you down himself and explicitly tells you to show Greg around. Now you've wasted Pastor's time and Greg's not impressed.

## C

Awkward silence. Pastor and Greg exchange a look. 'I'm just kidding,' you say. They let out nervous laughter.

'Here. Let me show you around!' You give him a brief tour before finishing up your tasks.

## D:win

Greg lets out the biggest laugh you've ever heard. 'You're wild,' he says. 'Now show me what you're working with here. Your pastor said you're doing a phenomenal job with what you guys have.'

## D:lose

You don't know why you said that. You immediately regret it. Now your pastor's embarrassed. Greg's embarrassed. You're embarrassed.

'Well.' Your pastor says. 'I need you to show Greg around. And let's have a chat this week.' He gives you a look then walks away.

You mumble to Greg while you give him a brief tour and feel like the worst person in the world.

Third song in. The room is engaged. You hit the bridge — the one you always blank on — and you either know it or you don't.

Jordan steps to her mic for her vocal part and she's crying. Not moved-by-the-Spirit crying. Sad crying. The congregation can't tell the difference. You can.

## A

She pulls through. After service she hugs you and says thank you. Greg says it was 'raw and real.'

## B

Smooth. Professional. Jordan notices and is either grateful or embarrassed — you'll never fully know which. After service she finds you. 'Thank you,' she says. That's all. You nod. That's enough.

## C

Bold pastoral move. Could be beautiful. Could be a 4-minute awkward pause. You step back from the mic. The room gets quiet. Something actually happens. Pastor is watching from the side of the stage. His expression is unreadable.

## D:win

Cody receives a signal. Cody is 17 and has been in this role for three weeks. Cody interprets the signal correctly — somehow. Jordan's mic drops in the mix. The arrangement covers her. Nobody in the congregation knows anything happened. After service DeShawn gives you a look. The look says: I saw that and I'm choosing loyalty over transparency. You nod. Nothing is ever said.

## D:lose

Cody receives a signal. Cody is 17 and has been in this role for three weeks. Cody interprets the signal as 'something is wrong with the mics' and lowers every fader on the board. The band is now playing at full volume to a silent auditorium. This lasts 11 seconds. It feels like a presidential term. When the sound comes back up Jordan's mic is louder than before. She is crying directly into it. After service the worship leader finds you and says, with the careful tone of someone selecting each word individually: 'What happened with the mics.' It is not a question.

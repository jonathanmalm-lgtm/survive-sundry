It's the third and final song. You've been prepping Jordan all week to lead what's meant to be the pinnacle moment of the worship set. She's prepared vocally. She's prepared in leadership. But not in emotions.

When the track counts in verse one…. Nothing. You look over to see Jordan stepped away from her mic, and she's crying. Not the overwhelmed by the goodness of God crying. The my boyfriend broke up with me on Friday and I finally can't handle all the build up of emotions kind.

It wouldn't be a big deal, but Greg is in the congregation watching to see what you'll do. Pastor's watching to see. The congregation's watching to see.

## A

Jordan pulls through. She uses the space to give what she's feeling to God and then leads the song with a depth of power you didn't know was in her.

After the set she hugs you and says thank you. Greg comes to find you both and says that was one of the most incredible moments he's experienced in worship.

Jordan tells you what happened with her boyfriend. You pull up his profile on Insta. Then you and Greg both take turns roasting everything he posts. Jordan smiles through teary eyes.

## B

You get through the song and through the set. Off-stage Jordan has regained her composure and apologizes for missing the moment. You pat her on the shoulder. 'Don't mention it.'

## C

You tried to say something heartfelt and profound. But you're a singer. Not a speaker. And what comes out is clunky at best. Heresy at worst. You see your pastor's wife turn to him and mouth, 'What is he even saying right now?'

It snaps Jordan out of her moment and she rescues you by singing the verse. She pats you on the back offstage with a pitying look.

## D:win

This song is one of your church's favorite songs. They sing it out in a roar. Jordan recovers quickly but keeps letting them lead. When the chorus kicks in, your and Jordan's voice join the choir and it's an incredible moment. You never hear them sing this loud outside of Easter and Christmas. In the congregation, Greg looks at you and nods his approval.

## D:lose

You highly overestimated your congregation. They barely know the song. Worse, Cody barely knows the song. He freezes and doesn't put the lyrics on the screen. A few people who know the song try to lead the rest, but you're pretty sure one of them is singing a completely different song.

Jordan never recovers. And even when you start singing, the rest of the song is a train wreck. The person giving announcements comes up and you have to sit there playing background music while the whole room feels incredibly uncomfortable.

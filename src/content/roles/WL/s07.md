You're offstage during the message. 28 minutes to hydrate, fix anything that broke, and not get cornered. You fail at the third one.

Ron. Eleven years at Execution Church. Ron stops you with the energy of a man who arrived at church today with purpose. His feedback is singular and specific: hymns. Real ones. Not the modernized versions with the Chris Tomlin chorus tacked on at the end. Not How Great Thou Art at 140 BPM with a key change. Hymns. From a hymnal. The way they were written.

Ron delivers his feedback. Then he pauses. 'I just want you to know,' he says, 'I'm only saying this because I care about this church.' Pause. 'I've been here eleven years.' Longer pause. He tells you what he tithes each month. It is your entire salary. From one person. You do not know what to do with your face right now.

He is looking at you. He is waiting. He would like a response. The sermon is happening fifteen feet away. Ron is not thinking about the sermon. Ron is thinking about hymns.

## A

Ron nods. Ron is not fully satisfied but Ron respects the diplomacy. He walks away. He is back next week with a follow-up. You think about the math again on the drive home.

## B

Ron does not know how to use the church app. You spend four minutes showing him in the lobby while the sermon happens fifteen feet away. This buys you a month. The math doesn't go away.

## C

Ron is briefly suspicious, then delighted. You have accidentally committed to a hymns Sunday. Not the Chris Tomlin versions. Ron will be very specific about this when he follows up on Monday.

## D:win

Ron goes very still. This is not the stillness of a man who is considering your point. This is the stillness of a man whose entire understanding of this conversation has just shifted. Then: he looks at you for a long moment. Then he almost smiles. Pastor Dave hears Ron out, asks you about it privately, and accepts your explanation with the weary patience of a man who has had this exact kind of conversation before. 'Don't do that,' he says simply. Ron receives a pastoral phone call that evening. Ron brings up Martin Luther for the next three Sundays unprompted.

## D:lose

Ron goes very still. 'I'm going to need to speak to Pastor Dave.' You say: 'Ron I was just—' Ron is already moving. Pastor Dave hears Ron out and then forwards you an email with the subject line 'Worship Direction — Let's Chat.' The email has no body text. Just a calendar invite for Monday morning. The invite has no title. Just a time. You spend Sunday night thinking about the hymns. The hymns are starting to sound pretty good right now.

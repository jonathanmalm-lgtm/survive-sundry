You're offstage during the message. 28 minutes to hydrate and fix stuff for second service. Now if you can avoid getting cornered by...

Ron. He was a founding member at Execution Church. He needs to talk to you. You aren't doing enough hymns. Real ones. Not the modernized versions where Chris Tomlin tacked on a monotonous chorus. No... He wants them the way they were written. All four verses of How Great Thou Art.

Ron gently reminds you that he's been at the church for fifteen years. He tithes every week. Ron isn't afraid to tell you the number. It turns out Ron tithes your whole salary.

Ron needs an answer now. He wants to know what you think.

## A

Ron nods. Ron is not fully satisfied but Ron respects the diplomacy. He walks away. He is back next week with a follow-up. Ron won't give up until you do.

## B

Ron does not know how to use the church app. You spend four minutes showing him in the lobby while the sermon happens fifteen feet away. This buys you a month. Ron will return with renewed vigor.

## C

Ron is overjoyed. He doesn't see the nuance of your agreement, only that you've committed to singing hymns on Sundays. When he plays golf with your pastor on Tuesday, he's going to tell your pastor about this conversation. Your pastor is now expecting you to start singing hymns too.

Guess your worship team is going to have to start learning to read music instead of chord charts.

## D:win

Ron goes still. He hadn't considered that things could get worse than they already were. Now Ron's arguing against using secular songs in the worship service. You finally relent and say he's right. You'll stick with the songs you've already been doing. Checkmate. You're a mastermind.

## D:lose

Ron goes still. 'I think I'll be talking to Pastor about this.' You say: 'Ron I was just—' Ron is already moving. Pastor Dave hears Ron out and then forwards you an email with the subject line 'Worship Direction — Let's Chat.' The email has no body text. Just a calendar invite for Monday morning. You spend Sunday night thinking about the hymns. The hymns are starting to sound pretty good right about now.

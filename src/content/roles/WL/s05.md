Two minutes to first service. The band is in position. Cody gives you a thumbs up from the booth. The room is filling up. The lights are doing the thing you like. For approximately fifteen seconds everything feels like it's going to be okay.

Then the iced coffee makes its decision known. Not a warning. A verdict. Your body has reviewed the milky iced coffee you consumed this morning and has issued a ruling that is both final and time-sensitive. You have two minutes until the start of first service. The bathroom is on the other side of the building and you're supposed to walk out in 120 seconds.

You have never led worship under these circumstances. Well, that's not entirely true. You have never admitted to leading worship under these circumstances.

## A

You cannot do this in two minutes. You know this before you start walking. You do it anyway. You return to the stage at 10:03 AM. The band has been vamping for three minutes. Cody has the lights on a slow fade loop that he invented on the spot. You walk out. You lead worship. Nobody knows except your team. Fortunately, they're loyal. (Except in the separate group chat they start to talk about this story.)

## B

You lead the entire first service but it's rough. Every song you sing sounds like you're trying to emulate Scott Stapp from Creed. Your eyes are closed tightly for 95% of the worship set. The people in the congregation exchange a few confused glances. But otherwise, you survive. Barely. As soon as you get off the stage you bolt to the bathroom and finally get relief.

## C

Jordan looks at you. You look at Jordan. Something passes between you that requires no words. Jordan takes the mic. Jordan opens the first song. Jordan is excellent. The congregation responds to her in a way that is genuine and slightly inconvenient for your ego. You return at song two. Greg finds you after service. 'Was that planned?' he asks. 'Of course,' you say.

## D:win

The whole operation takes four minutes. You have done things today that cannot be undone. You re-enter through the propped door, straighten your shirt, pick up your guitar, and walk on stage at 10:01 AM.

Nobody saw. It was an awkward start, but everyone blames that on Cody. You're in the clear.

The landscaping crew will discover an atrocity on Wednesday. But none of it will trace back to you.

## D:lose

The rock does not hold the door. You hear the click behind you and you know immediately and completely what has happened. You begin walking. Walking becomes jogging. Jogging becomes running because you can hear nothing from inside the building which means the congregation is sitting in silence. You push through the front doors. You pass through the lobby. You pass a first-time guest filling out a connection card. You arrive on stage breathing harder than a worship leader should breathe at the top of a service. 'Good morning,' you wheeze. You begin the first song. Nobody says anything. They also can't focus the whole service.

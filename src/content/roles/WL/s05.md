Two minutes to first service. The band is in position. Cody gives you a thumbs up from the booth. The room is filling up. The lights are doing the thing you like. For approximately fifteen seconds everything feels like it's going to be okay.

Then the iced coffee makes its decision known. Not a warning. A verdict. Your body has reviewed the milky iced coffee you consumed this morning and has issued a ruling that is both final and time-sensitive. You have two minutes to first service. The bathroom is on the other side of the building. Jordan is watching you from her spot on stage. Cody is watching you from the booth. Greg is somewhere in the room. You are the worship leader. You are supposed to walk out in 120 seconds.

You have never led worship under these circumstances. That is not entirely true. You have never admitted to leading worship under these circumstances.

## A

You cannot do this in two minutes. You know this before you start walking. You do it anyway. You return to the stage at 10:03 AM. The band has been vamping for three minutes. Cody has the lights on a slow fade loop that he invented on the spot. It looks intentional. It is not intentional. You walk out. You lead worship. Nobody knows. Cody knows. Cody will never say anything. You will never ask him to.

## B

You lead the entire first service with the focused intensity of a man engaged in a private spiritual battle that has nothing to do with the songs. Your eyes are closed more than usual. The congregation interprets this as deep worship. It is not deep worship. You make it. Barely.

## C

Jordan looks at you. You look at Jordan. Something passes between you that requires no words. Jordan takes the mic. Jordan opens the first song. Jordan is excellent. The congregation responds to her in a way that is genuine and slightly inconvenient for your ego. You return at song two. Greg finds you after service. 'Was that planned?' he asks. 'Of course,' you say.

## D:win

The whole operation takes four minutes. You have done things today that cannot be undone. You re-enter through the propped door, straighten your shirt, pick up your guitar, and walk on stage at 10:02 AM with the serene composure of a man who has made peace with his choices. Nobody saw anything. The bush is on the side of the building that faces the parking lot. Dave was not in that section of the parking lot. You are fairly certain Dave was not in that section of the parking lot. You lead worship. It is a good set.

## D:lose

The rock does not hold the door. You hear the click behind you and you know immediately and completely what has happened. You begin walking. Walking becomes jogging. Jogging becomes running because you can hear nothing from inside the building which means the congregation is sitting in silence. You come around the front of the building at a pace that Dave clocks from the parking lot. You push through the front doors. You pass through the lobby. You pass a first-time guest holding a connection card who watches you run by with an expression that will inform his understanding of this church for years. You arrive on stage breathing harder than a worship leader should breathe at the top of a service. 'Good morning,' you say. You begin the first song. Nobody says anything. Everyone is thinking something.

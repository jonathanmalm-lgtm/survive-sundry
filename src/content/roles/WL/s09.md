Second service. Bigger crowd. Higher energy. You feel it. The band feels it. Cody has added a light effect you didn't approve and it looks exactly as bad as something you didn't approve should look. You make a mental note. The mental note joins a list of mental notes about Cody that is getting long.

Front row, center: a woman is recording the entire worship set on her phone. Full brightness screen. Flash on. You aren't sure how her flash is brighter than the stage lights but it is. She is approximately eight feet from your face. You're seeing spots.

## A

You cannot fully close your eyes because her flash is basically a strobe. You get through it. You look mildly pained in her video which she posts to Facebook.

## B

She smiles back at you. It is the smile of someone who believes you are connecting with her about the worship experience. She keeps filming. You make the look again. She gives you a thumbs up. You have now made eye contact with her four times and she believes you are having a moment together. She pans the camera to include you. Your expression in the video is indescribable. She posts it: 'When the worship leader sees you capturing the anointing 🙌' It gets 340 views. Three people you know text you.

## C

The usher approaches during a quiet moment. It is not quiet enough. The congregation hears 'ma'am' and 'phone' and 'policy.' She is embarrassed. You feel guilty.

## D:win

She is too stunned to react for approximately four seconds. Then she starts laughing. Then the whole front section is laughing. That's such a {first name} thing to do. You finish the song. It is the loosest, most human moment of the entire morning. She posts the clip — all of it. It gets 47,000 views. The comments are entirely positive. Someone tags Brandon Lake. Brandon Lake does not respond. Your mom sees it and calls you. She thinks the article about you and Brandon Lake is now coming true.

## D:lose

She is too stunned to react for approximately four seconds. Then she posts the clip. All of it — the phone grab, the celebrity moment, the brightness adjustment, the handoff. It gets 47,000 views. The first comment is 'did he just TAKE HER PHONE' with 2,400 likes. The next 200 comments are a debate about whether this was appropriate. The campus pastor texts you during second service turnover: 'we need to talk about the phone thing.' Greg has already watched it twice.

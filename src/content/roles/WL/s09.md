Second service. Bigger crowd. Higher energy. You feel it. The band feels it. Cody has added a light effect you didn't approve and it looks exactly as bad as something you didn't approve should look. You make a mental note. The mental note joins a long list of points to give to the tech leader when they get back from vacation.

Front row, center: a woman is recording the entire worship set on her phone. Full brightness screen. Flash on. You aren't sure how her flash is brighter than the stage lights, but it is. She is approximately eight feet from your face. You're seeing spots.

## A

You cannot fully close your eyes because her flash is basically a strobe. You get through it. You look mildly pained in her video which she posts to Facebook.

## B

She smiles back at you. It is the smile of someone who believes you are connecting with her about the worship experience. She keeps filming. You make the look again. She gives you a thumbs up. You have now made eye contact with her four times and she believes you are having a moment together. She posts the video: 'When the worship leader gives you a shout-out from the stage 🙌' It gets over 10,000 views, mostly from people accusing your church of being too showy.

## C

The usher approaches during a quiet moment. It is not quiet enough. The congregation hears 'ma'am' and 'phone' and 'policy.' She is embarrassed. You feel guilty. But at least you can see again.

## D:win

The majority of the congregation break into a smile. That's such a {first name} thing to do.

A tension gets broken in the congregation and the worship experience in the second service is incredible for it. Plus the woman got a moment she'll forever cherish.

## D:lose

The woman is delighted. Your pastor and the rest of the congregation... Not so much. Your pastor texts you that he'd like to talk about what happened next week.

The woman posts the clip to Facebook. It gets 47,000 views. The first comment is 'did he just TAKE HER PHONE' with 2,400 likes. The next 200 comments are a debate about whether this was appropriate. The consensus is no.

Service has been over for twenty minutes. It's time for the Hits and Misses meeting. It's where the core team stands around and criticizes everyone else's department.

You have only an iced coffee and a granola bar in your system. Your stomach has its own criticisms of how the day went.

Pastor looks around at the group. 'Who wants to go first?'

Tom, the head usher, pokes his hand up and looks your direction. 'I want to be honest. I thought worship was a struggle today. It's usually a little bit better than that.'

His implication that it's never that good didn't slip your attention.

## A

You know what needs to change. And you don't need to throw anyone under the bus to fix it. 'It was a rough Sunday, but I have a plan of action to make sure it doesn't happen again.' Pastor nods. That's all he needed to hear.

A few more passive-aggressive misses are reported, then you break and are free to go. It's been a long day. You're glad it's over.

## B

It's true. There were a few great worship moments... At least at one of the services. Greg, who stuck around for the meeting, agrees with you. You glance at Greg gratefully.

Pastor nods and moves on.

A few more passive-aggressive misses are reported, then you break and are free to go. It's been a long day. You're glad it's over.

## C

A few more passive-aggressive misses are reported, then you break and are free to go. It's been a long day. You're glad it's over.

Now Tom's thinking about the chairs. You poked at Tom's greatest insecurity and derailed his criticism. Next week Tom arrives 30 minutes early to make absolutely sure the chairs are right.

A few more passive-aggressive misses are reported, then you break and are free to go. It's been a long day. You're glad it's over.

There are nineteen minutes between services. The band needs a reset, Cody needs reassurance, the stage has a water bottle someone left that will be visible on camera, and you still haven't eaten anything except that iced coffee, which was a mistake in more ways than one.

Your phone rings. It's your mom. She should know you're at work. Everyone in your life knows you're at work.

She probably wants to tell you about an article she saw on Facebook. Brandon Lake apparently sat down with President Donald Trump to discuss the current state of worship in America. She has several thoughts about what Brandon Lake said. The article is AI generated. You know this. You have told her how to spot AI generated content four times. It never sticks.

## A

She leaves a 5-minute voicemail. The last 40 seconds are her trying to figure out how to hang up.

## B

You were right. It's about a fake conversation between Brandon Lake and Donald Trump. It goes 12 minutes. You still haven't had a chance to eat so much as a donut. The day isn't getting better. But at least your mom's happy.

## C

She responds with 4 prayer hands emojis and 'Praying for your ministry. I'm proud of you...' It embarrasses you how good that makes you feel. You're a grown adult.

You reset the stage, fully focused. You are ready for second service.

## D:win

You interrupt your mom's explanation of the article (you were right). You explain that you're an adult and it's time she respect those boundaries. A moment of silence ensues. You're worried you came on too strong.

Finally she says, 'You're right. I'm sorry. I should have been more considerate. Call me when you get a chance. I'd love to catch up.'

It was a win. You still feel horrible, but it was a conversation that needed to happen. And now you can return to Sunday with a clear head.

## D:lose

You interrupt your mom's explanation of the article (you were right). You explain that you're an adult and it's time she respect those boundaries. A moment of silence ensues. You're worried you came on too strong.

Your mom starts bawling. She can't do anything right. You don't respect her.

You apologize for the next 17 minutes and almost miss the start of the next service.

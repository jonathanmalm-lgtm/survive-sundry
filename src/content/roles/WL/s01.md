You pull in and Dave is already there, setting out cones with the focused energy of a man who has found his calling. You watch him for a moment from your car. Dave is very serious about this. Dave has a system.

You have a thought you would never say out loud: people park themselves every day. At the grocery store. At the mall. At literally every other destination in their lives. Nobody puts on a vest and waves them in. They just… park. You are not sure what Dave is solving for. You feel guilty for thinking this. Dave is a volunteer. Dave showed up at 6:45 AM on a Sunday to put out cones. You get out of the car and tell Dave good morning and you mean it.

You have bigger concerns. You grab your guitar case, your laptop bag, your coffee tumbler, and your backup hard drive. Taped to the front door: a handwritten note. "Sound system has been acting weird. —Facilities." No name. No details. Just vibes.

## A

You have a relationship. She'll know what 'acting weird' means. Costs you 8 minutes of setup time but you walk in informed. The subwoofer blew overnight. The amplifier card is failing. You now have 90 minutes to solve a problem you didn't cause.

## B

You save 8 minutes but walk in uninformed. If it's bad, you'll find out the hard way. You will find out the hard way.

## C

He doesn't answer. You leave a voicemail with slightly too much urgency in your voice. He will bring this up later. You also learn, indirectly through a callback from his assistant, that the subwoofer blew overnight. You have 90 minutes to solve a problem you didn't cause.

## D:win

You've had the account for two years. 1,400 followers. Nobody at this church knows it's you. You caption it: 'when facilities leaves you a note that says acting weird and that's all the information you get 🙃' and post it before you're through the door. Nobody says anything. Today, anyway.

## D:lose

You've had the account for two years. 1,400 followers. Nobody at this church knows it's you. You caption it: 'when facilities leaves you a note that says acting weird and that's all the information you get 🙃' and post it before you're through the door.

What you do not know: Pastor Dave follows that account. Has followed it for eight months. He finds it relatable. He is scrolling it right now over his second cup of coffee. He sees the note. He sees the door. He recognizes the door. He does not laugh. He puts his phone down. He picks it back up. He looks at it again.

You will not find out he knows until the month-later meeting.

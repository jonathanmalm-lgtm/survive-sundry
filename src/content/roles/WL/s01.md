You pull into the parking lot of Execution Church. You've worked there the last three years. It's a pretty decent place, in spite of the questionable name. The only thing worse is the mission statement: "Make disciples or else..." Those three dots feel like a looming threat.

You forget all about it though with another sip of your iced coffee. Coffee makes everything okay.

You pass a parking lot volunteer setting out cones. As if people needed help figuring out how to park. It's always struck you as an unnecessary role but you'd never say it in staff meeting.

Anyways, you have bigger concerns. You grab your guitar case, laptop bag, and coffee. You walk to the door. Taped to the glass you see a handwritten note: "Sound system acting weird. - Facilities"

You aren't sure what that means, but it doesn't seem like good news.

## A

It costs you 8 minutes of setup time but you walk in informed. The subwoofer was on the fritz last night. You now have 90 minutes to solve a problem you didn't cause because of course it's on the weekend the tech leader is on vacation.

## B

You save 8 minutes but walk in uninformed. If it's bad, you'll find out the hard way.

## C

He doesn't answer. You leave a voicemail with slightly too much urgency in your voice. He will bring this up later. You also learn, indirectly through a callback from his assistant, that one of the subwoofers blew overnight. You won't be all about that bass this morning.

## D:win

You've had the account for two years. You have a whopping 1,400 followers. Nobody knows you run it. You caption the picture: 'vocalists making an excuse for why they can't hit the note 🙃' and post it before you're through the door. Risky move, but none of the people in your church saw the post.

## D:lose

You've had the account for two years. You have a whopping 1,400 followers. Nobody knows you run it. You caption the picture: 'vocalists making an excuse for why they can't hit the note 🙃' and post it before you're through the door.

What you don't know: Your pastor follows that account. He is scrolling it right now over his second cup of coffee. He sees the note. He sees the door. He recognizes the door. He does not laugh. He puts his phone down. He picks it back up. He looks at it again.

This might come back to bite you later.

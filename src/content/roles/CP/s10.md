After second service. You are in the lobby doing what you do — present, available, watching the room.

Chad finds you. Chad is a bartender at Chili's but he's very into finance. Chad has been making his way through your staff all morning. Chad has saved you for last because, he says, 'you're the decision-maker here.' Chad opens his phone. Chad has a deck. The deck is about BlippiCoin — a cryptocurrency backed by Blippi, the children's entertainer.

You have heard about BlippiCoin today already. It has come up twice. You know more about this than you expected to know.

Chad looks at you with the sincere hope of a man pitching the right product to the right person.

## A

'No thank you, Chad.' Chad receives this. Chad nods. Chad finds one more person in the lobby. That person is also on your staff. You make a note to address the BlippiCoin situation at the H&M meeting and do not do this.

## B

Chad's target demographic turns out to be: people who are open to risk, have some disposable income, and are looking for meaning in financial decisions. This is actually useful pastoral information. You take one page of mental notes. You also now know more about BlippiCoin than seems appropriate for a campus pastor.

## C

Chad listens to your explanation with the patience of a man who believes every 'no' is a 'not yet.' He thanks you for your time. He follows up twice. He is very polite about the follow-ups. You respond to neither follow-up. Chad remains a Chili's bartender.

## D:win

You have invested $200 in a cryptocurrency backed by a man in an orange and blue outfit who teaches children about excavators. You did this after preaching a sermon about faith. BlippiCoin is worth $124,000 by the month-later meeting. You have a story to tell. You will never be able to tell this story from the stage but you tell it at the elders' retreat and you tell it very well and everyone laughs for a long time. Blippi is completely unaware.

## D:lose

You have invested $200 in a cryptocurrency backed by a man in an orange and blue outfit who teaches children about excavators. You are the campus pastor. You did this on a Sunday. BlippiCoin is worth $0. You tell no one. Chad follows up twice. You do not respond. Three weeks later one of your elders asks if you've 'heard about that Blippi crypto thing.' You say: 'Briefly.' 'It's worthless,' the elder says. 'I know,' you say. The elder does not know that you know.

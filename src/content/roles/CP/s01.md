You arrive at 7:05 AM. Dave is already in the parking lot. Dave gives you a thumbs up. You give him a thumbs up. This is your entire relationship with Dave and it is a good relationship.

You are halfway to the door when Marcus stops you. Marcus is a congregant. Marcus is carrying a coffee from somewhere and has the expression of a man who has been holding a conversation in his head for two weeks and has decided that this morning, in this parking lot, is when that conversation happens.

Marcus's marriage is struggling. He says this quietly, standing between two parked cars, at 7:05 AM on a Sunday. He says: 'I don't know if this is the time.' It is not the time. He has already started talking. He is not wrong that he needs to say this to someone.

You have a full Sunday ahead. You also have Marcus.

## A

You are twenty-two minutes late to your first meeting. The team lead covers. The morning absorbs it. Marcus leaves service that morning looking lighter than he arrived. You schedule a proper appointment Monday. The Monday appointment leads to three more. The three more lead somewhere. You never fully know where because ministry doesn't have a final slide. It just continues.

## B

Marcus appreciates the structure. He knows you heard him. He emails you Sunday afternoon. The email is long and you read all of it. You respond Monday with a proper appointment. He comes. He is ready because the parking lot started something. The parking lot always starts something with you.

## C

Marcus looks at you. He says: 'Right, okay.' He goes inside. He sits in the second-to-last row, which is the row people sit in when they are not sure they want to be seen. He participates in service. He shakes your hand at the door. He does not make eye contact. He does not come back the following Sunday.

## D:win

You pray with Marcus in the parking lot. Dave is still there. Dave gives you a thumbs up from thirty feet away that suggests he cannot hear you but can see something is happening and endorses it. Marcus gets into his car after and sits in it for a moment. He comes inside. He sits in the third row. He comes back the following Sunday.

## D:lose

You pray with Marcus in the parking lot. Your prayer is genuine and good. It is also eleven minutes long. You are now twenty-eight minutes behind. The worship leader cannot reach you. The tech leader has a question that is time-sensitive. The administrator has a calendar issue. Dave is still giving you a thumbs up. Dave is the only person who is fully at peace with the morning.

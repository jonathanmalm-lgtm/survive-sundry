Second service. You're preaching. The livestream is up, or wasn't, or is back — the morning has been a lot and you've adjusted accordingly.

You're one minute from taking the stage. You are thinking about Greg's observation about first service energy. You are thinking about Marcus in the parking lot. You are thinking about the sermon you preached an hour ago and whether there is a better version of it available to you right now, with a full room that doesn't know about the subwoofer or the meme or the kid who got knocked over by a sound wave.

Second service is always its own thing. The people here didn't see first service. They don't know what the morning was. They just came to church.

## A

You open by telling the second service congregation about the livestream. You tell them what happened in the moment — the tech failure, the two-minute wait, the decision to walk out anyway. 'That's what we do here,' you say. 'We show up.' It lands. It's the best illustration in the sermon and you found it forty-five minutes ago in a crisis.

## B

The prepared version is good. You know this. The prepared work did what prepared work does — it shows up, it holds, it gives people something to take home. The second service is quieter than first service in a way you can't fully explain. Sometimes it is. That's okay.

## C

Announcements take six minutes instead of three. You use a joke that works better in the lobby than on stage. You feel this while you're saying it. The sermon starts late. It is good once it starts. The six minutes cost you the landing.

## D:win

You spend the sermon mostly in the center of the room, close. The two people in the front row — they don't know each other, both came alone, both sat near the front for reasons you'll never fully know — both stay afterward. One of them finds you. She says: 'How did you know?' You say: 'I didn't.' She looks at you. 'Okay,' she says. She comes back the following Sunday.

## D:lose

You spend the sermon focused on the front row. The front row feels this and responds well. Rows four through twelve are slightly adrift. Greg is in row four. Greg writes 'energy spread' in his notebook. You don't see this. Greg mentions it Thursday over lunch. You receive it. You think about it. You will preach to the whole room next week.

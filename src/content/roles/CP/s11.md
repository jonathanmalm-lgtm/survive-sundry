Services are done. The congregation is in the lobby. This is the third half-service — the twenty minutes of post-service conversation that is not scheduled but is essential. The sermon doesn't land until it's processed out loud with someone. You know this. You have always known this.

You also know that your wife is in the lobby, and she has been here since first service, and she has been on with your kids' school about something that has been pending since Thursday, and she gave you a look during second service that communicated several things without communicating any of them out loud, and that look is still in the room somewhere.

You are a pastor. You love this. You also have a family. You love that too. The lobby contains both of those truths simultaneously and they're both looking at you.

## A

You stay forty-five minutes. Your wife has handled the school thing. Your kids are with her in the lobby and they are fine. The family that stayed to talk with you — three of them — needed this conversation. You drive home together and stop for lunch. Your wife says: 'You were good today.' You don't know everything she means but you mean it when you say thank you.

## B

1:45 and you exit. Three people you didn't get to have conversations they were working toward. Two of them will come back to you this week in other ways. One of them won't. You'll never fully know which one. You eat lunch with your family. Your youngest does a bit about the worship leader's guitar that's very accurate. You laugh until it hurts.

## C

Ten minutes. Your office. You need it. You sit in the chair that faces the window. You don't look at your phone for the first time since 7:05 AM. Seven minutes in, someone knocks. It's Gary. Gary has something he'd like to go over before the H&M meeting. Gary has a list. It is not a short list.

## D:win

Marcus is near the back of the lobby. He sees you coming and does the thing people do when they're not sure if they're supposed to be found — he looks at his phone, then puts it down. You walk up. 'How'd it land today?' you ask. He says: 'Honestly?' You say: 'Always.' He tells you. It is the most honest thing anyone has said to you today. You file it.

## D:lose

Marcus is near the back of the lobby. You're halfway to him when three other people intercept you. You're with each of them for a genuine reason. By the time you look up Marcus is gone. You don't have his number. He submitted a connection card three weeks ago with a phone number you have in your system. You call Tuesday. He answers. He was glad you called. He needed more time anyway.

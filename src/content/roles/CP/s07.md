You are in the lobby during the message. This is where you go when you're not preaching — circulating, available, present for the people who aren't in the room.

Ron finds you. Ron has been here eleven years. Ron has feedback today that is specifically for you. The sermon this morning — and he begins here, which means this was prepared — is good, but the series has been running long and the congregation's engagement was 'visibly declining' by week four. Ron has been tracking engagement. Ron was not asked to track engagement. Ron tracked it anyway.

Ron tells you what he tithes each month. It is your entire salary. From one person. Ron wants you to know that. Ron is not delivering a threat. Ron is providing context.

## A

You give Ron nineteen minutes. The nineteen minutes are good. Ron says things that are accurate and things that are inaccurate. You receive both with equal grace. At the end Ron says: 'I appreciate you taking the time.' You say: 'I appreciate you caring.' You both mean it. You go home and look at the engagement data on your own. Ron was not wrong about week four.

## B

Ron receives the structure with the dignity of a man being respected. He says his piece in four minutes because you gave him a container. You repeat back what you heard. He nods. 'Monday,' he confirms. Monday's conversation is sixteen minutes and produces two things: a genuine insight about the series arc and a mutual respect neither of you would have named out loud but both of you felt.

## C

Ron notices. Ron finishes his thought but knows you were not fully receiving it. He says: 'I appreciate your time.' He walks away. The walk carries something Ron is not saying. Ron does not come back to you this week with follow-up. Ron's follow-up will arrive via email on Wednesday. Ron will CC Gary. Nobody asked Ron to CC Gary. Ron felt it was appropriate.

## D:win

Ron stops. Ron has not considered the elder board. Ron says: 'I wouldn't want to do that.' You say: 'Why not?' Ron says: 'Because then I'd have to stop giving feedback informally and start giving it formally, and I'm not sure that's better.' You look at Ron for a moment. 'Fair point,' you say. Ron nods. 'I'll schedule something for Monday,' Ron says. This is the most useful thing Ron has ever said to you in the lobby during a sermon.

## D:lose

Ron looks at you for a long moment. Ron says: 'Is this because of what I said about the series?' 'No,' you say. 'It's about your longevity and investment in this church.' Ron says: 'I'd like to think about it.' Ron leaves. Ron calls the board chair on Monday. The board chair calls you Monday afternoon. 'Did you ask Ron to join the elder board in the lobby during the sermon?' 'I raised the possibility,' you say. 'On the same morning you told him the series was running long?' The board chair is quiet. You are quiet. 'Let's talk Wednesday,' he says.

The team huddle is at 9:00 AM. 47 people. Lobby. Standing. You run this thing.

The First Impressions leader gives you a look that means the staffing is thin this morning. You already knew. You factor it in.

One of your team leads steps forward and gives the 'we get to do this' speech. She builds to a Scripture reference — 1 Corinthians 10:13. 'God won't give you more than you can handle.' She delivers this with the authority of someone landing a truth bomb. The verse is about resisting temptation. You know this. Three of the people in this room know this. No one says anything. You nod. This is also part of the job.

You produce the $5 Chick-fil-A gift card. This was decided at staff meeting. Your team nominated the volunteer most visibly on the edge. There was a consensus, which there always is. His name is Kevin. Kevin arrived at 6:15 AM, is running two roles because the second person no-showed, and has said 'we're good, I got it' approximately eight times this morning. He receives the card with the expression of someone who didn't know they were being watched but is very glad they were. The room feels it. These are the moments the building is for.

Then you introduce Greg. Former worship pastor from a well-known church that recently imploded. You invited him. You told him you wanted him to see something healthy. He is looking at the room with the eyes of someone doing a recalibration.

## A

The huddle is four minutes. Clean, warm, specific. Two people tell you after service it was the best huddle they can remember. You don't remember which two because you were already moving, but one of them was Greg.

## B

The huddle runs six minutes. People leave it with something. Two people are slightly late to their stations. Both of them run their stations well. The six minutes were the right call.

## C

The team lead runs it. She is good. She is not you. The room gets something different this morning and they're not sure what the difference was. Dave, who attends every huddle from the parking lot door, gives a half thumbs up. You don't know what a half thumbs up means. You've never seen it before.

## D:win

'We're short. God is not. Let's go.' The room is quiet for one second. Then someone says 'let's go' back. Then two people say it. Then it's the whole room. Greg says 'I haven't seen that in a long time' afterward. He means the room saying it back. He does not mean you said it first.

## D:lose

The delivery lands a little harder than you intended. One team lead looks at you with the expression of someone trying to determine if this is a declaration or a prayer request. Another team lead checks their phone, which you notice. The moment recovers. Greg looks at you after and says: 'Short and direct. Good instinct.' He pauses. 'I'd practice the landing.'

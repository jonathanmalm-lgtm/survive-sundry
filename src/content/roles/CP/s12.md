Twenty minutes after second service. You run this meeting. Everyone is standing. Nobody has eaten since the huddle donuts four hours ago. This is not a meeting with food. This is a meeting where you find out what people actually thought.

You know more about this Sunday than anyone in this room. You were in the parking lot with Marcus at 7:05. You were in the booth. You were in the lobby. You were on stage. You were in the hallway when Ron found you. You were at the door when Greg was leaving. You know the wins and the misses from every angle.

Gary is positioned near the front of the group. Gary has been waiting for this meeting since 8:47 AM.

## A

You go first and you go long — not long-long, but complete. You name the livestream as a miss and the response as a win. You name the lobby coverage gap. You name Marcus by first name without context — 'A conversation in the parking lot this morning that reminded me why we do this' — and you see three people in the room straighten slightly when you say it. Gary opens his mouth. He closes it. That's enough.

## B

Thirty seconds. One win, one miss. You hand it to the team. They go around. By the time it gets back to you you've heard seven different angles on the same Sunday. You close with two sentences. One of them lands. Greg, if present, writes something.

## C

The team lead goes first. She's good. The room follows her lead instead of yours. You go when it comes to you. You have your thirty seconds. It's fine. It's not your best. Gary goes before you and sets a tone that you then respond to, which is the wrong order.

## D:win

The team talks for twelve minutes. You learn three things you didn't know about your own Sunday. One of them is from the kids ministry leader. One of them is from the worship leader. One of them is from Gary, which is the most surprising. Gary's observation is accurate. You make a note. You close the meeting. 'Same time next week.' Four seconds. Gary moves first. You watch him go. You are already thinking about Monday.

## D:lose

The team talks for twelve minutes. Then it comes to you. You have too much. You try to give it structure but the structure doesn't hold because there is too much morning in the way. Gary has by now given two observations and is working toward a third. The campus pastor ending the meeting last means the meeting ends on whatever the last person said before you, which was Gary's third observation, which was about the haze.

The meeting ends on the haze.

'Same time next week.'

Four seconds.

Gary moves first.

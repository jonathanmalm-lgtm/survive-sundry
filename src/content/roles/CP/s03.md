You are in your office. The door is closed. There is a paper taped to the outside: 'It's show business, not show friends.' You put it there. You believe in it. This morning it also functions as a boundary.

You have a sermon to review. You have check-in texts to send to three team leads who are managing significant situations this morning. You have a Greg situation you are managing relationally. You have a budget question from an elder that arrived at 7:47 AM on a Sunday and should not have been sent at 7:47 AM on a Sunday.

You have forty-five minutes before sound check and the beginning of your pastoral morning. You have enough material for about sixty-five minutes of work.

## A

The sermon is as ready as it's going to be. You know which parts land and which parts need to land differently. You make three small edits. The third edit is the best thing in the sermon. You wouldn't have found it without the forty-five minutes.

## B

You send three texts. Two of them are responded to immediately. One is not responded to until the huddle, at which point you already know the situation has been handled because Dave told you in the parking lot. Dave always knows. The sermon is adequately reviewed. You find the third edit during the sermon, out loud, which makes it feel more spontaneous than planned. It was neither.

## C

You review the sermon in the car. There is a traffic delay. The traffic delay is eleven minutes. You do not find the third edit. The sermon is good. You know it was supposed to be better. You know where the better part was supposed to go. The congregation does not know. The congregation finds it good. You are the only one sitting with the knowledge of what it could have been.

## D:win

You've preached this structure before. Not to this congregation. The bones are right. The specific illustrations are wrong — you've updated them but not deeply enough. In service one there is a moment where you start a sentence and realize, mid-sentence, that you have told a version of this story at your previous church. You adjust in real time. It works. Nobody in the room knows. You know.

## D:lose

You've preached this structure before. Halfway through service two you tell an illustration that you prepared for this congregation and realize, mid-illustration, that you told a nearly identical illustration in service one, which means you are now telling it twice. Three people notice. Two of them are your staff who were at both services. One of them is your elder board chair, who attended both services because of Greg. After service he finds you. 'Was that illustration different in first service?' he asks. 'Slightly,' you say. He nods. He writes nothing down. He doesn't have to.

Turnover between services. You find Greg in the lobby, which is where you'd been expecting to find him.

Greg is ready to share. Greg is a professional at this by now — he's been through enough churches in enough capacities to know how to give feedback that is both honest and kind. His opening is: 'You've built something real here.' His middle includes three specific observations with concrete examples. His close includes one thing he'd 'watch.' He delivers all of it with the care of someone who knows that feedback is a form of investment.

The one thing he'd watch: your preaching is good, but first service had a different energy than second service. He's not sure why. He thinks it might be the tech situation. He thinks it might be something else.

## A

You take three pages of notes. You ask about the second service energy observation specifically — what did he see? He tells you. It's specific and it's accurate and it's something you've felt but haven't named. Greg says: 'Most people in your seat can't hear feedback. You can.' You accept the compliment. You don't fully trust it yet. You're working on that.

## B

Greg receives the deferral graciously. He says: 'I'll write it up if that's easier.' He sends a three-page document Monday. You read it three times. It is the most useful external perspective you've received in two years.

## C

Greg receives the positive warmly because he is perceptive enough to know that the deferral of the harder part is where the real information is. 'Let's schedule it,' he says. The lunch happens Thursday. The harder feedback lands harder on Thursday than it would have on Sunday, because by Thursday you've had four days to convince yourself you already know what he's going to say. You don't fully know what he's going to say.

## D:win

Greg is quiet for a moment. He says: 'Tell me more about what that would look like.' The conversation that follows is forty-five minutes. Greg is not available — he has his own season to process. But Greg knows someone. Greg makes an introduction Friday. The introduction leads to something. Dave somehow knows about the introduction before you tell him. Dave gives you a thumbs up.

## D:lose

Greg says: 'I appreciate the thought.' He pauses. 'I'm not in a place to take something on right now.' You receive this. Then Greg says: 'I also want to be honest — I came here to give you a gift. Asking me to work for you changes what the gift is.' You sit with that for a moment. 'Fair,' you say. Greg nods. 'The feedback stands,' he says. 'I know,' you say. You mean it more than you did before he said that.

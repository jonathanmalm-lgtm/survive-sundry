You get to the staff area and immediately feel it. The specific atmospheric pressure of two people who had an exchange in the last twelve hours that was not fully resolved and who are now in the same room on a Sunday morning pretending it was resolved.

The worship leader and the administrator. They were both here last night for the late stage reset. Something happened during that reset — not a blowup, something smaller, the kind of small thing that can become a big thing if it isn't addressed, and neither of them has addressed it because it's Sunday morning and there's too much else.

They are both doing their jobs. Their jobs are slightly louder and slightly more deliberate than usual. You notice this.

## A

Three minutes. You close the door. You say: 'Something happened last night and I need both of you to be fully present today.' You don't know what happened. You don't ask. You say: 'Whatever it is, it waits until Monday. Can you give me today?' They both say yes. The yes from both of them is genuine. Monday the conversation happens. It is less difficult than Saturday night made it seem.

## B

You check in with the worship leader first. He says 'I'm fine.' You wait. He says: 'We'll talk Monday.' You say: 'Yes you will.' You find the administrator. She says: 'We'll handle it Monday.' You say: 'Good.' They both do their jobs well that morning. There's still some atmospheric pressure. There is less of it.

## C

The atmospheric pressure is present throughout the morning. By service one it has stabilized into professionalism. By service two it is genuinely fine. Monday's conversation gets pushed to Tuesday. Tuesday gets pushed to Thursday. Thursday they handle it themselves without you. You find out it's resolved when the administrator sends the worship leader a calendar invite for the following Saturday's stage reset. He accepts.

## D:win

Your general principle lands. Both people hear it. Neither of them looks at the other. Both of them look at you in a way that suggests they know what you're doing. The principle is about team unity before Sunday. It's not wrong. It's not subtle. It works. Monday's conversation happens because they both felt the nudge.

## D:lose

Your general principle lands publicly on three other people who have no idea what it refers to. All three of them assume it's about them. Two of them find you after the huddle. 'Is everything okay with my team?' one asks. 'What did I miss?' asks the other. You spend seventeen minutes reassuring people who were not the intended audience. The worship leader and the administrator watch this happen. They are both trying not to look at each other.

There was a moment in first service. The worship leader, mid-song, stepped off the stage and walked into the congregation. He didn't plan it. He just did it. The congregation parted slightly. He kept singing. Someone near the third row raised their hand. The lighting happened to be doing something very specific at that angle.

You missed the shot. You were repositioning. By the time you had the frame the moment was already resolving.

Second service is about to start. The worship leader is in the wings. The moment might happen again. It might not. Moments that happen once sometimes happen twice. Moments that are manufactured look like moments that were manufactured.

## A

You think through it — the angle, the light, the likely position. You station yourself at the spot during the first song. The moment doesn't happen the same way. A different moment happens instead. You get that moment. It's good. It's not the first service moment. It's its own thing. That's the job.

## B

Second service has a different feeling than first service. You find three things that are specific to this service and don't appear in any of your previous content. One of them is Greg's face during the second song. You don't post the Greg photo. But you see it. You'll remember what you saw.

## C

You position yourself at the same spot and wait for the same moment. The worship leader doesn't step off the stage in second service. He does something else instead — something less visual but more present. You get the shot you were waiting for, which is a shot of a moment that isn't happening, which means you get nothing. You post filler content.

## D:win

The worship leader looks at you. He looks at you for a moment. Then: 'What do you mean by the thing with the crowd?' You try to describe it. He listens. He says: 'I can't do that on purpose.' 'Right,' you say. 'That's a problem.' 'Yeah,' you say. He looks at you. 'Okay, I'll see what happens.' In second service he doesn't do the thing. He does something else. You get that instead. It's different and honest and you post it.

## D:lose

The worship leader's face when you ask him. He says: 'Can I do what?' You explain. His face moves through several states. 'I can't recreate that,' he says. 'It just happened.' 'I know,' you say. 'I was thinking—' 'No,' he says, very clearly. He walks onto the stage. You go find your spot. The moment doesn't happen in second service. You post filler content. The worship leader avoids you at the H&M meeting.

You arrive and the shot is right there.

Dave is in the parking lot setting out cones. The morning light is doing something real — long shadows, golden tone, a man with a vest and a purpose. You have your camera out before you fully register reaching for it. You frame it. It's good. It's actually good. It's the kind of photo that people share without being asked.

Dave has not seen you. Dave is entirely unselfconscious. Dave is also a volunteer, and you have a general policy about posting volunteer photos without permission, which you wrote yourself, and which you are currently weighing against the fact that this photo is really good and the light is leaving.

## A

You post it before you're through the door. The caption is three words: 'The work begins.' It gets 247 likes by noon. Dave never mentions it. Dave does not use Instagram. Dave knows you posted it because Dave knows things. Dave gives you a thumbs up the following Sunday that has slightly more weight than usual.

## B

You ask Dave. Dave looks at you. Dave looks at the camera. Dave says: 'Sure.' Dave positions himself in a way that suggests Dave has thought about how he'd look in a photo and has a preference. It's still a good photo. Different from the candid but good. Dave approves the final before you post. Dave gives it a thumbs up. Not on Instagram. In person. Thumbs up.

## C

You keep looking. The light is gone by the time you find another shot you feel good about. You post something from last week's service as a filler. The filler does its job. The Dave photo stays in your camera roll. You look at it twice during the day. It is still very good.

## D:win

The caption about authentic community gets forty-seven comments. Two of them are from people who didn't come this Sunday but say 'this is why we love this church.' One of them is from someone who attended for the first time this morning and felt 'like they were expected.' Dave's thumbs up the following Sunday has additional weight. You still don't know what Dave knows.

## D:lose

Dave sees the post. Dave is not on Instagram. Dave's daughter is on Instagram. Dave's daughter shows Dave the post. Dave doesn't say anything about the caption. Dave doesn't say anything about the photo. Dave does say, the following Sunday, that he'd 'prefer a heads up' next time. You cannot tell if Dave is hurt. Dave does not give you a thumbs up that morning. You feel the absence of the thumbs up more than you expected.

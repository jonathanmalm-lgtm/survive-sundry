Third song. The room is engaged.

Jordan, the acoustic guitarist, steps to her mic. She's crying. You can tell from thirty feet away. Not moved-by-the-Spirit crying — something else. She keeps playing. She keeps singing. The congregation reads it as worship. You read it as a person in pain who is also professional and also choosing to keep going.

Your camera is in your hands. You have the shot.

## A

You take three frames. They're honest. Jordan's face in the third frame is something you'll think about for a long time — the specific expression of someone who decided to be present for something despite themselves. The campus pastor sees them on Monday. He's quiet. Then: 'These don't go anywhere.' 'I know,' you say. 'Keep them,' he says. 'Okay,' you say. You keep them.

## B

You lower the camera. You watch. Three people near you also notice Jordan. One of them has their hand on their chest. The room holds something that no photo could hold. After service you find Jordan at the merch table. You don't mention the camera. You ask how she's doing. She looks at you. 'How'd you know?' she asks. 'I was watching,' you say. She nods. That's the whole conversation.

## C

You have the photo. It sits in your camera roll for three weeks. You look at it sometimes. You never post it. You never delete it. At some point you show it to the worship leader, who shows it to Jordan, who says she'd like to have it. You send it to her. She doesn't say anything else. You don't need her to.

## D:win

You post it during service. Forty-three comments in twenty minutes. Most of them are 🙏 and 'this.' Two of them are from people who say it's why they came that morning. Jordan sees it at turnover. She looks at you. 'I didn't know you were going to post that,' she says. 'Is it okay?' you ask. She looks at it for a moment. 'Yeah,' she says. 'It's okay.' You file the conversation carefully.

## D:lose

You post it during service. Jordan finds out at turnover. She is not okay with it. She is very not okay with it. She is professional and controlled and very not okay with it. The worship leader finds you during second service setup. He says: 'Take it down.' You take it down. Jordan says nothing else to you today. The campus pastor sends you a 🙂 at 12:15 PM. You know what that emoji means now.

You are in the lobby during the sermon. You've shot four things in the last twenty minutes, one of which is very good and three of which will be used as filler content.

Ron finds you. Eleven years at Execution Church. Ron has feedback about the church's social media presence. The feedback is specific. There are too many photos of the worship team. There are not enough photos of the congregation. There are zero photos of people reading their Bibles. Ron reads his Bible every morning. Nobody has ever asked to photograph that. Ron has a printed list. The list has categories.

Ron tells you what he tithes each month. It is your entire salary. From one person. Ron would like you to know that. Ron is waiting for a response.

## A

You read the printed list later. Item three is actually a real insight about community over production. Item seven mentions 'spiritual disciplines content' which you have a Pinterest board for and have never used. Ron is occasionally ahead of the curve on things that are not hymns.

## B

Ron listens. Ron disagrees with the strategy in the specific way of someone who has thought about this differently. 'The strategy is working,' he says, 'but I'm not sure it's working for the people who have been here the longest.' You sit with that on the drive home.

## C

You disengage in ninety seconds. Ron walks away. Ron sends a written summary to the campus pastor by 4:47 PM. The summary is organized into five categories. Category four is 'Suggested Photo Series.' The campus pastor forwards it to you Monday. 'Some good ideas in here,' he says. You read the summary. Ron is not wrong about category four.

## D:win

Ron stops. Ron has thought about this too. 'What would that look like?' he asks. You describe it — longtime members, specific places in the building, their story. Ron says: 'I'd be willing to do that.' Ron means it. You schedule it for the following Saturday morning. Ron shows up seven minutes early. Ron is the best subject you have photographed in three years. He knows exactly where to stand.

## D:lose

Ron receives the pitch with the careful consideration of a man who is evaluating whether he is being managed. 'You want to photograph me?' he asks. 'You and others,' you say. 'Others,' Ron says. He looks at you. 'Let me think about it,' Ron says. Ron goes home. Ron calls the campus pastor Monday. 'Your social media person wants to photograph me.' The campus pastor says: 'Is that okay?' Ron says: 'I'm not sure yet.' You receive a 🙂 from the campus pastor at 2:15 PM Monday.

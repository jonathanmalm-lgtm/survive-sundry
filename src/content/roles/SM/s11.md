Services are done. You are going through your camera roll when a woman finds you. Her name is Diane. Diane is a regular. Diane is the kind of regular who always has the same seat and the same coffee and the same quiet presence week after week.

You photographed Diane this morning. Diane didn't know. The photo is candid — she's in the third row with her eyes closed, hands open, very present. It's one of the best shots of the morning. You posted it forty minutes ago. It has 140 likes.

Diane would like it taken down. She doesn't want her photo on social media. She's been coming to this church for four years and has never been photographed and that has been intentional. She says this calmly and clearly and without anger. She is looking at you.

## A

'I'm so sorry, Diane. Taking it down right now.' You pull out your phone. You delete the post while she watches. It's down in forty-five seconds. Diane says: 'Thank you.' That's the whole thing. You add Diane to your no-photo list. You create a no-photo list. Diane's row will never be in your frame again. Diane comes back next Sunday.

## B

You hear Diane's four-year history with this church. You hear why she values anonymity. You take the post down during the conversation, before you've finished listening, because you realize somewhere in the third sentence that there's no universe in which the photo should stay up. Diane notices that you took it down before she finished. 'Thank you for not waiting,' she says.

## C

Diane looks at you. 'I don't have Instagram,' Diane says. 'A friend showed me.' You take the post down. The tag offer was a mistake and you both know it. Diane says: 'Okay.' She leaves. Diane comes back next Sunday. She sits somewhere different. You notice. You don't know what to do with that information.

## D:win

Diane looks at you for a moment. Then: 'I understand the context.' She takes a breath. 'I'd still like it down.' 'Of course,' you say. You take it down. The conversation continues. Diane, it turns out, spent twelve years doing communications for a nonprofit. She has thoughts about the Sunday strategy that are specific and useful. You take notes. You schedule coffee Thursday.

## D:lose

Diane's expression changes. Not to anger. To something quieter. 'It's not about the likes,' she says. She walks away. She does not come back next Sunday. The campus pastor calls you Monday. He has talked to Diane. He does not raise his voice. He says three things. You will remember them precisely. The third one is: 'The photo was good. The decision was not.'

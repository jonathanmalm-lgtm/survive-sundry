Turnover between services. You posted a worship shot during the first service with a caption about community. It performed well — good engagement, good energy, several shares.

Then a comment arrived. The comment says: 'I was here this morning and nobody talked to me.' It has four likes. All four are from other people who have apparently had similar experiences. The comment is not hostile. It is factual and sad.

You are holding your phone in the lobby during turnover and the comment is on your screen.

## A

You write: 'Thank you for being here. We're sorry that was your experience. We'd love to connect with you — please DM us or reach out to our welcome team.' You send it. Three people see it and like the reply. The person who left the comment DMs you. They're named Sarah. You connect her with the First Impressions leader. Sarah comes back the following Sunday.

## B

You DM: 'Thank you for trusting us with this. We're sorry. Can we connect you with our team?' Sarah responds within twenty minutes. She was still in the parking lot. She comes back inside. She goes to the second service. She is greeted at the door. She sits with someone who introduces herself.

## C

The comment disappears. Sarah notices her comment is gone. Sarah posts a new comment: 'They deleted my comment.' The new comment gets nine likes in eleven minutes. Three of them are from people who weren't at the church. The campus pastor calls you at 12:07 PM. You miss the call. He texts: 'Call me.' You call.

## D:win

You like the comment. The like says: we saw you. The person who left it looks at the like for a moment and then DMs you. 'Did someone actually see this?' 'Yes,' you say. 'I wasn't sure if anyone would,' she says. The conversation continues from there.

## D:lose

You like the comment. The four people who already liked it see your like. One of them adds a reply: 'At least someone noticed.' The comment thread has grown. The campus pastor sees it. He does not text you a smiley face. He texts you a question mark. Just a question mark. You have learned that a question mark is worse than a 🙂

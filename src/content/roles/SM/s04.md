The team huddle is at 9:00 AM. The tech leader steps forward and acknowledges the staffing gaps this morning — you're short. But — she pauses — 'God won't give you more than you can handle.' 1 Corinthians 10:13. You know that verse. You've used it in a caption once. Looking at it now it might have been the wrong verse. You file this.

The campus pastor produces a $5 Chick-fil-A gift card. Staff consensus. He calls Nina's name. Nina handles the first-time guest table and has been having quiet conversations with first-timers since 8:15 AM with the focus of someone who knows this is the most important thing happening in the building right now. She receives the card with the expression of someone who didn't know they were being watched but is very glad they were.

Then Greg is introduced. Former worship pastor. Well-known church. Imploded. Good jacket. Very good jacket. You are already thinking about the jacket. Greg's former church had a very specific aesthetic — clean, high-contrast, intentional. You have studied that church's Instagram. You studied it two years ago, before everything, for reference. Greg is wearing the aesthetic.

## A

Greg is genuinely useful. He talks about curation vs. documentation, which is a distinction you've been trying to articulate for months without finding the right words. He used the word 'editorializing' in a way that clarifies something for you. You exchange information. He sends you two articles Thursday. They're good articles. You don't tell anyone where you found them.

## B

You stay professional. Greg mentions the social media from his former church in passing. You catch it. You file it. After service you look up his former church's account. It's archived. You stare at the archived account for longer than you should. It was genuinely beautiful work.

## C

Greg receives the introduction warmly. He doesn't know what you manage until you tell him. When you tell him he looks at you differently — not condescendingly, more like: 'oh, you're the person who knows things I don't.' 'Can we talk sometime?' he asks. 'Yes,' you say.

## D:win

Greg laughs. 'Sure,' he says. You take the photo. It is an excellent photo. Greg sees it when you show him and says: 'Hm.' The 'Hm' is a positive hm. You post it with 'Our guest this morning, Greg' and a very short caption. The campus pastor's response is: '😊' Full smile emoji. The comments include two people who recognize Greg from his former church and are happy to see him doing well.

## D:lose

Greg receives the request with the slight stiffening of someone who is navigating a very specific tension about what his public presence means right now. 'Maybe not today,' he says. The campus pastor sees the exchange from across the lobby. He finds you after the huddle. 'We don't need to document Greg,' he says. 'I know,' you say. You both know you know. The knowing doesn't make it better.

Twenty minutes after second service. Everyone is standing. Nobody has eaten since the huddle donuts four hours ago. This is not a meeting with food. This is a meeting where you find out what people actually thought.

Campus pastor goes first. Win — both services. Miss — he was in the lobby and missed the check-in window with the team. He looks around.

Before your turn Gary the head usher says: 'I just want to be honest, I noticed the photographer was in the aisle during first service.' Gary was in his spot during first service. Gary's spot is at the back. Gary saw you walk down the aisle. Gary has been sitting with this for two hours.

## A

You go. The room listens. When you get to Diane the campus pastor looks up. He nods once. The nod is affirmation that you named it, not celebration that it happened. Gary says: 'I just want to be honest, I was wondering if there was a consent issue.' You say: 'There was. I handled it.' Gary nods. The campus pastor moves the meeting forward.

## B

Clean. The campus pastor receives all three parts. At the fix he says: 'Good.' Gary says: 'What's the consent policy?' The campus pastor says: 'That's part of the fix.' Gary nods. Gary accepts this. This is the best Gary has ever interacted with a process improvement.

## C

The campus pastor writes something. He already knows about Diane — Diane called him. He doesn't bring it up in the meeting. He brings it up Monday. The Monday conversation is direct and professional and not what you wanted to have on a Monday. The metrics were good though. This does not help.

## D:win

The room receives the metrics with the energy of people who are glad something is going well. Then the campus pastor says: 'The Diane situation?' He already knows. He wants you to name it publicly. You name it. 'Got it,' the campus pastor says. 'Fix?' 'Consent protocol,' you say. 'Good,' he says. Gary starts to say something. The campus pastor raises a hand. 'Same time next week,' he says.

## D:lose

The campus pastor says: 'Diane called me.' The room goes quiet. You say: 'I handled it.' He says: 'I know. But 140 likes is not the frame for that conversation.' The room has heard something now. The room will remember the 140 likes. You will also remember the 140 likes. The number will feel different for a while.

Around the room: Tech reports clean. Worship says second service had something. Kids ministry says Jackson is coming back next week. That's the win and the miss. First Impressions says the door felt warm but the lobby felt cold. Nobody claims the lobby. Someone says the haze was a lot today. Nobody claims the haze.

'Same time next week.'

Four seconds.

Gary moves first.

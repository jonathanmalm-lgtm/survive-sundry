The worship leader finds you before you even get your camera out of the bag. He has a list. The list is specific — he wants a photo of the full band from the back of the room, a close-up of the in-ear monitors on the stand, and a wide shot of the stage before people arrive.

The list is from last week's request. You recognize two of the angles. They were good last week. They will be fine this week. They are also very much the kind of photo you post when you don't have anything better.

You also see: the drummer's kit is set up in a different position. The confidence monitor has something on it that is briefly, visually, perfect before Cody fixes it. Patricia is tuning an oboe in the corner and the morning light is behind her and it is something.

## A

You follow the list. The photos are fine. The confidence monitor moment passes. Patricia's oboe moment passes. The drummer's new position produces photos that are slightly different from usual in a way nobody will notice. The content is serviceable. The campus pastor says 'good work' at the H&M meeting. You accept this. You think about Patricia.

## B

You deliver the shot list and add fourteen additional photos. The campus pastor receives a content dump on Monday morning. His response is: '🙂' followed by 'Let's talk about curation.' The curation conversation happens Tuesday. You learn something useful. You also have fourteen really good photos.

## C

The campus pastor responds immediately: 'Shoot what you see. The list was a starting point.' You feel a small amount of relief and a large amount of 'I already knew this.' You shoot the morning. Patricia and the oboe is the best photo of the day.

## D:win

You shoot the morning freely. Patricia and the oboe is the best photo. The drummer's new position produces an angle you haven't used before. The content tells a story that a shot list couldn't have predicted. The campus pastor sees the Patricia photo and says: 'Who is that?' 'Patricia,' you say. 'She plays in the church orchestra?' 'She plays the oboe.' He looks at the photo. 'Post that one,' he says.

## D:lose

You shoot the morning freely. You don't deliver the shot list items. The worship leader asks about the band photo at the H&M meeting — he'd promised it to someone. You don't have it. You were standing next to the band all morning. You have forty-seven other photos. You don't have the one you were asked for. The worship leader nods. He says: 'That's okay.' He means it but you hear something else.

Jackson is here.

You said this at the H&M meeting three weeks ago. 'Jackson is coming back.' The room received those words with the gravity they deserved. Today is the day. Jackson is eight years old and has the energy of a Labrador retriever who has consumed a significant amount of birthday cake. Jackson has been asked to leave two previous churches' children's programs. You do not know what happened at those churches. You can imagine.

Jackson's mother drops him off with the smile of a woman who is deeply grateful that this is your problem for the next ninety minutes.

## A

Jackson is suspicious at first. Then he is all in. His job: hold the door for the pre-schoolers. Jackson holds the door with the focus of a Secret Service agent. Three pre-schoolers walk through. Jackson says 'welcome' to each one. You feel something real. Then Jackson holds the door for a 4th grader and says 'you're too big for this door' and you have about forty seconds before the 4th grader cries.

## B

His parents come out from the service. The look on their faces is complex. They take Jackson to the lobby. You do not know what happens in the lobby. Jackson comes back in eleven minutes. He is slightly calmer. Slightly.

## C

The 5th grade room handles it for fourteen minutes. Then they text you. The text is one word: 'help.'

## D:win

Jackson grips the clipboard like it is a sacred object. He uses the walkie-talkie to announce things to no one in particular. He walks with the authority of a man in charge of something important. He doesn't break a single thing. After service his mom finds you: 'What did you do?' You say: 'Gave him some responsibility.' She starts crying. You were not expecting that.

## D:lose

Jackson has a clipboard and a walkie-talkie. Jackson has identified four additional problems using the walkie-talkie. Jackson has assembled a small committee of second-graders to address these problems. The committee is now in the supply closet. You are not sure how they got in. The supply closet locks from the inside.

You got here at seven because you are that person. Coffee in hand, lanyard already on, mental checklist already running. The kids wing is yours. You know every corner of it. Which is why, the moment you push open the double doors, you stop.

There is a smell.

Not a faint smell. Not a "maybe someone left a snack bag" smell. This is a biblical smell. This is a smell that has opinions. It's coming from somewhere near the two-year-old room, and it arrived before you did, which means it has been here long enough to get comfortable.

You have two hours before families start walking in with their actually-two-year-olds.

## A
You follow your nose like a bloodhound with a theology degree. Three minutes later you find it: a diaper pail that was never emptied last Sunday, sealed shut by heat and ambition. You tie it off, take it out, and douse the room with the lavender spray you keep in your cabinet for exactly this kind of emergency. The smell does not win. You win. Room is ready by 7:22 and you feel genuinely heroic about it.

## B
You light every battery-powered candle in the wing. You crack two windows and prop open the fire door with a chair (which you know you shouldn't do). You say a quiet prayer that is mostly just the word "please" repeated several times. By 9:45, the smell has mellowed into something you'd describe as "rustic." One parent wrinkles their nose. You tell her it might be the new cleaning solution. You are lying.

## C
You text Marcus from custodial: "Hey! Good morning! Not sure if you've been by the two-year-old room yet, but there might be something that needs attention when you get a chance — no rush, just wanted to flag it! You're the best!" Marcus is not on call until 8:30. He reads it at 8:47. He responds: "on it!" He arrives at 9:15. The smell has by now achieved personhood.

## D:win
You take one photo — just enough to document — and drop it in the all-staff chat: "Two-year-old room. Help?" Marcus arrives in eleven minutes because he respects urgency delivered visually. The campus pastor replies with a thumbs-up emoji, which you interpret generously. Room is cleared, sprayed, and fully operational by 8:00. Dave from the parking lot texts: "Heard about the smell. Respect." You don't know how Dave knows things. Dave just knows things.

## D:lose
The photo is more graphic than you intended. Dave from facilities says it looks like a crime scene. The campus pastor forwards it to the elder board with the subject line "Facilities Concern" before you can explain context. By 7:45 you have received three replies from elders you have never personally met. The smell is still there. You are now also managing a small governance situation before service.

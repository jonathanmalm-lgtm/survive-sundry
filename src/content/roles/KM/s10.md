Service is running. You are in the hallway between the toddler room and the 3-year-old room. You have approximately ninety seconds before someone needs something.

Chad finds you. Chad is a bartender at Chili's but he's very into finance. He's been making his way through the staff. He found you specifically because — and he says this with complete sincerity — 'you know more about Blippi than anyone here, and this is a Blippi-based investment opportunity.'

He is not wrong. You know more about Blippi than you ever intended to know. You have watched Blippi explain excavators forty-seven times across three service years. Chad has a deck. The deck is about BlippiCoin — a cryptocurrency backed by Blippi, the children's entertainer.

## A

Chad retreats. He goes back into the lobby. A four-year-old rounds the corner at full speed. You step aside. You are very good at this.

## B

3 minutes becomes 9. A volunteer has to come find you. Jackson has been unsupervised for 7 minutes. Jackson has been busy.

## C

Chad sends a 14-paragraph email at 11:45 PM with the subject line: 'THE FUTURE — for your eyes only.' You forward it to the worship leader because you think he will enjoy the Blippi angle. He does.

## D:win

You have invested $200 in a cryptocurrency backed by a man in an orange and blue outfit who teaches children about excavators. You have watched that man explain construction vehicles for three years. BlippiCoin is worth $124,000 by the month-later meeting. You tell no one for a week. Then you tell the worship leader. He had an opportunity too. His face when he hears this is genuinely worth $124,000.

## D:lose

You have invested $200 in a cryptocurrency backed by a man you have watched explain garbage trucks to four-year-olds. BlippiCoin is worth $0. You tell no one. Chad follows up twice. You do not respond to Chad. Blippi is completely unaware that any of this happened. Next Sunday you will watch Blippi explain excavators again. You will feel a feeling you cannot name.

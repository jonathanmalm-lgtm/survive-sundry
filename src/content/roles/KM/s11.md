Service is over. Parents are coming to pick up. Jackson is currently organizing the art supply closet in a way that does not match any system you have ever established. He is doing this with complete confidence.

Jackson's mom finds you before you find her. She looks like she's been in a worship service for ninety minutes but also like she has been braced for a conversation at pickup for ninety minutes. These two things have created a complicated expression.

She says: 'So — how was he today?'

## A

She listens. When you get to the supply closet she turns around and looks at Jackson. Jackson has created a filing system based on color. Not labeled color. Just color. He has reorganized the yellow things. He is now reorganizing the blue things. Jackson's mom turns back to you. 'Thank you for not giving up on him,' she says. Her voice catches on the last word. You were not prepared for that.

## B

She nods. She already knows what 'great' means in this context. 'Can we talk this week?' she asks. 'Yes,' you say. 'Thursday?' 'Thursday.' You make the call Thursday. It is the best pastoral conversation of your month.

## C

She knows. She has been doing this long enough to know. She takes Jackson's hand. Jackson says goodbye to the supply closet. She looks at you once more and you see a woman who is very tired and is also very used to being very tired. You feel something about this on the drive home.

## D:win

Jackson's mom stares at you for a moment. Then: 'He would absolutely love that.' She gets out her phone. She is writing this down. 'Clipboard?' she asks. 'Clipboard,' you say. 'And a walkie-talkie?' 'Critical.' She finishes the note. She looks at Jackson reorganizing the supply closet. 'I'm going to try this at home,' she says. 'Start small,' you say.

## D:lose

Jackson's mom says: 'We have a walkie-talkie.' Pause. 'He broke it in three days.' You absorb this. You look at your walkie-talkie. You look at Jackson. Jackson has moved from blue things to the scissors and is testing their mechanism with the focused curiosity of an engineer. You quietly take the scissors.

Turnover between services. You are resetting the lobby, briefing the second-service team, and preparing for the second wave when a man stops you.

His name is Kevin. Kevin is a regular. Kevin says, very directly, that he brought his coworker this morning — a first-time guest — and nobody spoke to them when they came in. They sat in the back. Nobody sat near them. Nobody spoke to them at the end of service either. His coworker's first impression of this church was: 'There were a lot of people there.'

Kevin is not angry. Kevin is a problem-solver. Kevin would like this to be better. Kevin is telling you because he believes you can fix it. This is actually a gift. It doesn't feel like a gift right now.

## A

Kevin appreciates being heard. Kevin is the kind of person who doesn't need a solution in this moment — he needs to know the problem was received. You receive it. 'What's your coworker's name?' you ask. Kevin says 'Marcus.' You write it down. You find Marcus in the lobby. You talk to Marcus yourself. Marcus comes back the following week with Kevin. Kevin gives you a thumbs up.

## B

Kevin respects the directness. You tell him you'll move one more greeter to the section where he sat. You find out where he sat. You adjust the positioning for second service. After second service a volunteer reports that a first-time couple in that section said 'everyone here is so warm.' You file this. Kevin did you a favor.

## C

Kevin receives the explanation. Kevin understands the explanation. Kevin was not asking for an explanation. Kevin nods politely and moves on. His coworker is currently deciding whether to come back to this church. He comes back once more. He comes back to a different section of the auditorium, which has better coverage. He is greeted warmly on that second visit. He joins a small group six weeks later. You will never know this happened.

## D:win

Kevin looks at you. Kevin is a systems person. Kevin sees that you're also a systems person. Kevin says: 'Yeah. I'll do that.' Kevin comes to the H&M meeting. Kevin gives direct, specific, useful feedback. The campus pastor listens. Gary says: 'I just want to be honest, I think Kevin makes a fair point.' This is the first time Gary has been fully helpful. Nobody reacts. You file it.

## D:lose

Kevin is not a church staff person. Kevin was not prepared to attend a Hits and Misses meeting. Kevin says: 'Oh — I can come to a meeting?' You say: 'Please.' Kevin comes. The team receives Kevin's feedback with varying degrees of grace. Gary says: 'I just want to be honest, this is unusual for a guest to be at this meeting.' Kevin is now a guest who has been told by Gary that his presence is unusual. Kevin finishes his feedback and leaves. Kevin does not come back the following week.

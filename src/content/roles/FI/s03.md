The lobby is your canvas. You have seen what it looks like when this room works — when the flow is right and the greeters are positioned and the first-time guest table is visible and the connection cards are where people can actually find them. You have also seen what it looks like when it doesn't.

Right now it is not fully either. The chairs near the welcome area are blocking the hallway to the bathrooms. The haze from last week's service has left a faint visible layer near the ceiling that nobody has mentioned and everyone can see. The connection card holder is empty because Gary refilled it with the wrong year's cards — they say 2023. It is not 2023.

You have 90 minutes. First guests arrive in 45.

## A

You find seven things to fix. Two of them require Facilities. Two of them you handle yourself. Two you delegate. One — the haze — you note and accept. You cannot fix the haze. No one can fix the haze.

## B

The high-traffic areas are ready by 8:45. Two smaller things go unaddressed. One of them will be noticed. One won't. You spend the rest of setup time with your team, which means your team is ready when the doors open.

## C

The welcome table looks excellent. A first-time guest asks where the bathrooms are. You direct her around the chairs you didn't move. She navigates them successfully. Two other guests try the same path and one of them bumps a chair. Nobody falls. Nothing breaks. Gary says 'I just want to be honest, I've always thought those chairs were in the wrong place.' Gary is correct. You knew this.

## D:win

Gary takes the clipboard. Gary is extremely thorough. Gary discovers three things you missed. Gary documents them in a format that has subcategories. Gary updates the lamination schedule. This is the most productive 45 minutes Gary has ever contributed to your ministry. You don't know how to feel about this.

## D:lose

Gary takes the clipboard. Gary uses the clipboard to make organizational decisions that are not within the scope of what you asked Gary to do. Gary moves three things. Gary tells two volunteers to move two other things. By the time you complete your walk, the lobby has been reorganized according to Gary's internal logic. Gary's internal logic is not your logic. Some of it is correct.

You arrive at 6:45 AM. Dave is already here. Dave is always already here.

Dave is your longest-tenured parking lot volunteer. Dave has laminated maps. Dave's maps show parking flow, cone placement, and directional arrows with a specificity that suggests Dave may have surveyed this property with professional equipment at some point. You have never asked. You do not want to know.

This morning, Dave is waiting for you with a new laminated map. The new map has a second entrance configuration that Dave worked out 'based on historical data.' Dave is very serious about this. Dave says 'historical data' with the confidence of someone who has a spreadsheet. Dave probably has a spreadsheet. You have not seen the spreadsheet.

Your existing volunteers are looking at you and then at Dave and then at you. Some of them have worked with the old map for years.

## A

The transition has some friction. Dave walks two volunteers through the changes. One volunteer disagrees with the new entrance configuration. Dave explains the historical data. The volunteer accepts this. Dave nods. You realize Dave has been waiting to use this map for approximately three weeks and was never going to stop waiting.

## B

Dave understands. Dave folds the new map carefully. Dave puts it in his vest pocket. Dave will bring it up again in approximately seven days. You will be ready.

## C

Two parking sections use the old map. One uses the new map. One volunteer follows neither and operates on instinct alone. Seventeen cars are parked in an arrangement that will make leaving after service a significant experience for everyone involved.

## D:win

Dave watches you present the map. Dave's expression is unreadable. After service Dave finds you. 'You presented it well,' Dave says. That is all Dave says. Dave laminated another copy. He gives it to you. 'In case you need a reference,' Dave says. You will never fully understand what Dave knows.

## D:lose

Dave knows. Dave knows immediately. Dave's expression does not change but something shifts behind it. Dave helps you anyway. Dave is a professional. After service Dave finds you. He does not raise his voice. 'You might want to put my name on the attribution next time,' Dave says. He hands you his laminated copy. He already made another one.

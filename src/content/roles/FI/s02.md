Three greeters didn't show. You have a Planning Center page that says fourteen people confirmed. You have eleven people standing in the lobby. No texts, no calls, no explanations. Just absence.

Your secondary entrance near the gym has no coverage. Your first-time guest table has one volunteer who is also supposed to be doing welcome packets. Gary is at the main door. Gary arrived early and organized the bulletins in a way that is slightly different from how you asked him to organize them. Gary believes his organization is more intuitive. Gary may be right. You don't have time to evaluate this.

The first cars are already pulling in.

## A

Two people respond within six minutes. One is a regular attender who always says yes. The other is a student ministry volunteer who cleared his schedule 'just in case.' You don't know how he knew. You have never asked. He shows up in jeans and a hoodie and greets people with the natural warmth of someone who has not been briefed on anything and doesn't need to be.

## B

You spend three hours at the main door. Your clipboard stays tucked under your arm the whole time. You make eye contact with every single person who walks through. First-time guests get thirty seconds of focused attention from you specifically. You are exhausted. You are also the reason several of those families come back.

## C

Nobody responds to the all-staff chat for eleven minutes. When someone does respond, they ask if you've tried the backup list. You had not yet tried the backup list. You try the backup list.

## D:win

You flag down forty-three cars. You are very enthusiastic. Three first-time guests later report that 'the man outside' was the reason they came back. They describe you as 'the most welcoming person they'd ever experienced at a church.' Dave clocks your location from across the lot and updates the map.

## D:lose

You flag down forty-three cars. You are very enthusiastic. On car twenty-seven you flag down someone who was not coming to church. He was turning around in the parking lot. He is now parked. He is in service. He stays for the whole thing. He gives his life to Christ. This should feel like a win. It does not fully feel like a win.

Second service is running. The lobby is in good shape. You have twelve minutes before you need to be anywhere specific.

Chad finds you. Chad is a bartender at Chili's but he's very into finance. He has been working his way through the staff all morning. He found you because — and he is very direct about this — 'you manage the front of house and I want to talk to you about a front-of-house opportunity.' Chad opens his phone. Chad has a deck. The deck is about BlippiCoin — a cryptocurrency backed by Blippi, the children's entertainer. Chad has been told by someone — you do not know who — that you are 'the decision-maker for this area.'

Chad is wrong about what you make decisions about. Chad's confidence is unaffected by this.

## A

Chad nods. Chad finds the campus pastor in the lobby. The campus pastor listens to the pitch for four minutes before extracting himself with the pastoral warmth of a man who has escaped harder conversations. He finds you later. 'Who's Chad?' he asks. You explain. The campus pastor nods. He says: 'He had a deck.' 'He always has a deck,' you say. The campus pastor considers this.

## B

The lobby does not manage itself. A greeter has to handle two things without you. One of them works fine. The other one produces a situation that you have to address after service. You also now know more about BlippiCoin than you expected to know.

## C

Chad sends the deck at 11:58 PM. The deck is 14 slides. Slide 7 is titled 'LOBBY SYNERGIES.' You do not know what lobby synergies means in the context of a Blippi-based cryptocurrency. You forward it to the kids ministry leader. She responds: 'Why.' You respond: 'Slide 7.' She responds: 'Oh.'

## D:win

You have invested $200 in a cryptocurrency backed by a man in an orange and blue outfit who teaches children about excavators. You did this in a church lobby while managing first impressions. BlippiCoin is worth $124,000 by the month-later meeting. You tell exactly one person. They are quiet for a long time. Then: 'Tell me more about this Chad.'

## D:lose

You have invested $200 in a cryptocurrency backed by a man in an orange and blue outfit who teaches children about excavators. BlippiCoin is worth $0. You tell no one. Chad follows up twice. Chad will stop finding you in the lobby and will start emailing you. You do not respond to Chad's emails. Chad is still a bartender at Chili's.

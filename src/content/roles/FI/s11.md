Services are done. A volunteer finds you with his phone turned outward. He is showing you something. He has the expression of someone who does not want to be the one showing you this but feels it should not wait.

There is a Google review. Two stars. Posted forty-three minutes ago — meaning during second service. The review reads: 'Big church, didn't feel welcome. Nobody said hi. Coffee ran out. Couldn't find a seat.' The reviewer's name is 'K. Morris.' The profile photo is a blurry photo of a dog.

Some of this is your responsibility. Some of it is not. All of it is your problem.

## A

You write the response. You get it to ninety percent, then share it with the campus pastor before posting. He changes three words. You post it. The response is warm and specific and not defensive. K. Morris does not respond. One other reviewer, unprompted, adds a five-star review that says 'best lobby I've been in.' The campus pastor shows this to the elders. Gary says he's been here eleven years and it's always been welcoming. He is not wrong.

## B

You look through connection cards from this morning. You find a 'K. Morris' on a card from second service. You call the number on Monday. She answers. She is surprised you called. The conversation lasts twelve minutes. She comes back the following Sunday. She leaves a four-star review. The one-star upgrade feels like a win. It is a win. Dave saw her leave the first time. Dave sees her come back. Dave updates the map.

## C

The campus pastor receives the flag Monday morning. He is not upset. He asks what happened with the coffee. You explain. He asks about the greeting gap. You explain. He asks you to handle the response. He trusts you with the response. You write a good response. You wish you had written it on Sunday when you had the context fresh.

## D:win

K. Morris responds: 'Lol okay.' This is not clearly positive. K. Morris updates her review to three stars. You do not know why. You do not ask. The campus pastor sees the reply and says: 'The door thing was a risk.' 'I know,' you say. 'It landed,' he says. He walks away.

## D:lose

K. Morris responds: 'This seems like something you're told to say.' She updates her review to one star. She adds a sentence: 'They replied but it felt scripted.' The campus pastor reads the exchange. He is quiet for a moment. 'What does 'closes slowly by design' mean?' he asks. You explain about Ron. He stares at you. 'Same time next week,' he says.

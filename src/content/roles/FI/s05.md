Eight minutes to first service. You are managing three things at once and doing it well when you see them.

A family. Dad, mom, two kids under ten. They're standing near the entrance with the specific stillness of people who have not been to this church before and are waiting for someone to tell them what to do. The dad is holding a connection card that he got from a greeter outside. He doesn't know what to do with it. The mom is scanning the room. The kids are watching other kids run by with the body language of children who would very much like to be running.

This family has driven here this morning. You don't know how far. You don't know what they're carrying. You know they're here, and nobody has made eye contact with them in approximately ninety seconds.

## A

You walk them in. You find out they have an eight-year-old and a five-year-old. You walk the kids to children's check-in personally. You introduce the parents to a second-year attendee who has a similar-aged family. They sit together. They come back the following week. Dave from the parking lot sees them park and gives you a thumbs up. Dave knows. Dave always knows.

## B

Your best greeter is on it in forty-five seconds. She has a gift for this. She walks them in, connects the kids, and finds them seats with a family she knows will be warm. After service the mom finds your greeter: 'Thank you for not just pointing.' Your greeter finds you to tell you this. You both nod. That's what the door is for.

## C

They find the welcome table. The welcome table has a volunteer who is also managing three other things. The family gets a bulletin and a parking map. The kids find children's ministry on their own. Service starts and the parents are still in the lobby. They sit in the back. They don't come back the following week. You don't know they don't come back. Dave knows. Dave does not tell you. Dave updates the map.

## D:win

Greg is excellent with them. Genuinely excellent. He has a child the same age as their oldest. He walks them all the way to children's check-in and then walks the parents to a seat. After service they tell the campus pastor that 'the man in the jacket' was the reason they felt welcome. The campus pastor relays this to Greg. Greg is quiet for a moment. Then: 'Good.'

## D:lose

Greg is warm with the family but also, at some point during the lobby conversation, mentions that he used to lead worship at a well-known church. He says this in the past tense, carefully. The dad recognizes the church name. The dad has read about the church. The dad has read about the pastor. The conversation shifts. The family is fine. The moment is complicated. You will not know about this until someone mentions it at the H&M meeting in a way that requires context.

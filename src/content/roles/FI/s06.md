Service has started. The lobby is supposed to be clear. The lobby is not clear.

There is a line at the coffee bar. Not a short line. A committed line. Eleven people, some of them in their church clothes, some of them in their everyday clothes, all of them standing in the lobby while service is happening inside the auditorium. The coffee volunteer ran out of cups twenty minutes ago and was waiting for someone to bring more. Nobody brought more. The cups are in the storage room. The storage room was locked. The key was on a hook that Gary moved 'for organizational purposes.'

Gary has been in service for eight minutes. Gary does not have his phone.

## A

You find the emergency supplies — paper cups from the kitchen — and open the supply cart as a second station. The line moves in four minutes. Two people from the line come to you after service to thank you. One of them is a regular. One of them is the first-time family from this morning. The dad is holding a paper cup. He gives you a thumbs up. You give him a thumbs up.

## B

You pour coffee for seven people. You are the First Impressions Leader and you are pouring coffee in the lobby during service. This is exactly the job. You find the cups in the storage room with a key from the maintenance closet. The line clears by 10:38. You smell like coffee for the rest of the day.

## C

The sign goes up. People stand there and read the sign. Two of them leave. Three wait. One photographs the sign and posts it on Instagram Stories with the caption 'lol.' The caption has no church tag but Dave's laminated map is visible in the background. Dave sees the post. Dave does not like the post. Dave documents the post.

## D:win

The eleven people in line receive no coffee. Eleven of them go inside. Eight of them find it genuinely fine. Three of them are annoyed for the rest of service. None of them say anything at the door. One of them leaves a Google review that is two stars. The review says: 'No coffee. Nice people though.' This is technically a win.

## D:lose

One of the eleven people in line is a visiting pastor from a church in another state. He is here to evaluate the campus for a potential partnership. He stands in the lobby for seven minutes reading the sign. He goes inside. He does not have coffee. His write-up of the visit later mentions 'operational gaps in the lobby experience.' The campus pastor asks you about the coffee bar at the H&M meeting. Gary says: 'I just want to be honest, I moved the key for organizational purposes.'

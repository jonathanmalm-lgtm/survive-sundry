Second service. The auditorium is full. Not full-ish. Full. There are people standing near the back. There are people standing in the lobby watching through the glass doors. Two ushers are doing the math and looking at you.

The overflow room — a side room with a monitor feed — is available. The monitor feed was working last week. You have not checked if the monitor feed is working this week. The overflow room also has chairs in it but they are not set up for overflow. They are set up for the marriage counseling class that meets Thursday and Gary has not moved them.

Gary is inside. Gary is in his usual spot. Gary has not been informed that overflow is needed.

## A

You find two volunteers and get the room set up in eight minutes. The monitor feed works. Twenty-three people use the overflow room. Four of them prefer it. Two of them say it's quieter. One of them has a newborn and cries with relief when she sees the monitor. You add overflow readiness to your pre-service checklist.

## B

You work the lobby personally for eleven minutes, keeping people comfortable while your team sets up overflow. One family leaves. You get their name from the visitor card they filled out. You follow up Monday. They come back the following week to a seat you have personally ensured will be available. Dave sees their car and updates the map.

## C

Gary receives the information. Gary processes the information. Gary has a process for overflow. Gary's process takes eleven minutes and involves Gary reorganizing the chairs in a sequence Gary finds intuitive. Gary's sequence is not wrong. Gary's sequence is just slower than the situation requires. People are standing in the lobby. Three of them leave before overflow is ready.

## D:win

The campus pastor receives your text. He responds during the sermon in a way that suggests he checked his phone during his own sermon, which is a whole other conversation. His response: 'Your call.' You make the call. The call is correct. After service he finds you. 'Good call,' he says. 'You texted during your own sermon,' you say. He looks at you. 'Same time next week,' he says.

## D:lose

The campus pastor does not respond during service. He responds at 12:47 PM: 'Sorry, just saw this — how did it go?' You had already made the call. The call was fine. But you waited for an answer that didn't come, which cost you six minutes, and six minutes at capacity produces two families who leave and three people who stand in the lobby deciding whether to stay. One stays. Two leave. Dave updates the map.

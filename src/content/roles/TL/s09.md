Second service. Worship is running. The room is engaged. You are monitoring levels, watching the stage, staying ready.

Then the booth door opens. It's the pastor's kid. He is nine years old. His name is Caleb. Caleb has been in the booth before. Caleb likes the booth. Caleb likes buttons. The booth has many buttons. Caleb sits down in the empty chair next to you like a man arriving at his office. He puts his feet up on the edge of the console. He looks at the board. 'What does this one do?' he asks. His hand is already moving toward a fader.

## A

Caleb stays. Caleb is mostly fine. Caleb asks 14 questions during the remaining worship songs. You answer 11 of them. The other three you answer with 'not right now Caleb' which Caleb accepts with the patience of a child who knows 'not right now' is not a no.

## B

Caleb looks at you. Caleb has his father's eyes. Caleb leaves without argument. After service the pastor mentions casually that Caleb said you kicked him out of the booth. 'I redirected him,' you say. 'He said kicked,' the pastor says. He is smiling. The smile is real. You make a note to use the word redirected going forward.

## C

Caleb takes this responsibility extremely seriously. The haze machine during second service produces more haze than the combined total of every previous service this year. The stage disappears. The worship leader disappears. The front three rows disappear.

## D:win

Caleb finds a fader that feels satisfying to move and moves it. You are now in a fader negotiation with a nine-year-old during second service worship. You also handed him a battery. You win the fader negotiation. Eventually. Caleb is back the following Sunday. Caleb heads directly for the booth. He asks if you have more batteries. You do not tell him how many you have.

## D:lose

Caleb wins the fader negotiation. The volume during the last worship song is loud enough that two people in the front row put their fingers in their ears. Ron puts his fingers in his ears. Ron looks at the booth. Ron sees Caleb. Ron's expression shifts from irritated to confused. Ron will mention the child at the booth in his feedback this week. This is the first time Ron has ever been correct about something you also agree with.

Eight minutes to first service. Everything is as ready as it's going to be. DeShawn has the lyrics fixed. Kayla's situation is whatever it is. Marcus has declared the mix 'honest.' You have learned not to ask what that means.

The kids ministry leader appears at the booth door. The check-in printer in the kids wing isn't working. Parents are already arriving. She needs it fixed now. She is looking at you because you are the tech person. You are not that kind of tech person. You run audio, lighting, streaming, and ProPresenter. You do not run inkjet printers manufactured in 2014 that are connected to an iPad via Bluetooth and have a paper jam that may or may not also involve a spiritual component. You know this. She knows this. She is looking at you anyway.

## A

You leave the booth 8 minutes before first service. DeShawn is now unsupervised. Marcus is now unsupervised. You fix the printer in 6 minutes by turning it off, removing a piece of paper that has been in the feed tray since what appears to be the Obama administration, and turning it back on. You return to the booth. Something has changed in the booth. You don't know what yet. You will find out during the first song.

## B

This takes 9 minutes and involves you describing printer parts to someone who is also managing 12 arriving children simultaneously. It works. Eventually. The first two families sign in on paper. One parent asks if the paper sign-in is more secure. You do not have time for this question.

## C

She does not love this answer. She accepts this answer. You feel mildly guilty for exactly 90 seconds and then first service starts and you feel nothing except focus.

## D:win

She stares at you. She takes the pen. She does not laugh. She does not not laugh. It is somewhere in between and you cannot read it. She leaves. DeShawn is looking at you. Marcus is looking at you. Neither of them says anything. After service she finds you in the booth and hands the pen back without a word. You take it without a word. You both nod. Something has been communicated. You are not entirely sure what.

## D:lose

She stares at you. She takes the pen. At the H&M meeting her miss is the printer. She holds up the pen when she mentions it. She looks at you. The room looks at you. The campus pastor looks at you. 'We'll get the printer fixed this week,' you say. 'Great,' he says. He writes something down.

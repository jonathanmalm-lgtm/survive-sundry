The team huddle is at 9:00 AM in the lobby. You are here because it is mandatory. You are mentally still in the booth.

The First Impressions leader steps forward and acknowledges what everyone already knows — you're short-staffed this morning. The holes are real. But — and here he pauses for effect — 'God won't give you more than you can handle.' He says this with the confidence of someone citing Scripture. He is citing Scripture. The verse he is thinking of is 1 Corinthians 10:13, which is about resisting temptation, not running four tech areas with three people.

The campus pastor produces the $5 Chick-fil-A gift card. This was decided at staff meeting — the team quietly nominated whose volunteer was most visibly on the edge. It wasn't a long discussion. There was a consensus. He calls Bri's name. Bri drove 40 minutes to be here, is covering two roles because nobody else signed up, and has been smiling hard enough that you almost missed the exhaustion underneath it. She receives the card with the expression of someone who didn't know they were being watched but is very glad they were.

Greg is introduced. Former worship pastor at a well-known church that recently imploded because their pastor had a moral failure. He is here to observe and encourage. He is wearing a very good jacket. Greg looks at your booth setup notes on the table and says, with genuine warmth: 'Oh, you're in tech? We had just upgraded our whole system. Dante network, full fiber runs, the works.'

## A

Greg is actually very helpful. You get three genuinely useful tips. You also spend 11 minutes in a tech conversation while your volunteers are unsupervised in the booth.

## B

Professional. Clean. Greg thinks you're focused and impressive.

## C

Greg is genuinely impressed. He asks if you've ever considered consulting. You will think about this for several days.

## D:win

The room goes quiet. Greg's smile stays exactly the same size for slightly too long. Then Greg laughs. Eventually. It takes four seconds but it arrives and when it does it's a real one. 'No,' he says. 'Dante is an audio networking protocol.' 'Right,' you say. 'Of course.' After service he finds you in the booth. 'That was the worst and best question anyone has asked me all year,' he says. You take this as a win.

## D:lose

The room goes quiet. Greg does not laugh. The campus pastor physically stands up straighter in the way of a man activating a professional setting he reserves for specific situations. The huddle moves on. Three volunteers near the back are visibly trying not to make eye contact with anyone. The campus pastor finds you during turnover. He says three sentences. You remember all three of them for the rest of your life.

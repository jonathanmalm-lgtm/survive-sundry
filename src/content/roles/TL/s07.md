Sermon is running. You step out of the booth for the first time since 6:52 AM to find water and possibly a moment of peace. You find neither. You find Ron.

Ron has been attending Execution Church for eleven years. Ron stops you with a specific energy — not angry, just prepared. His feedback today is technical in nature. The mix was too loud. The bass was too heavy. The lighting during the second song was 'disorienting.' He produces a folded piece of paper from his shirt pocket. It is a printed list.

Ron tells you what he tithes each month. It is your entire salary. From one person. You do not know what to do with your face right now. He is waiting for a response.

## A

You read it. Item 7 is actually a legitimate note about a feedback loop that you've been meaning to fix. Ron is occasionally correct.

## B

Ron does not know how to use the church app. You spend 6 minutes showing him. It goes to the general inbox. Nobody monitors the general inbox.

## C

Ron looks at the list. Ron looks at you. Ron takes the list back. Ron folds it carefully and puts it back in his shirt pocket. 'I've been here eleven years,' he says. He walks away. The list goes back in the pocket. The list will return.

## D:win

Ron is now wondering if he has been emotionally manipulated into his tithe via volume levels for eleven years. Ron takes his list back. He walks away slowly. He is thinking. Ron will research this. Ron will find conflicting information online. Ron will arrive next Sunday with printed articles.

It is not a warm same time next week.

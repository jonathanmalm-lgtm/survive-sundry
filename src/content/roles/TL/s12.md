Twenty minutes after second service. Everyone is standing. Nobody has eaten since the huddle donuts four hours ago. This is not a meeting with food. This is a meeting where you find out what people actually thought.

Campus pastor goes first. Win — both services ran. Miss — he was in the lobby talking to people during turnover and missed his window to check in with the team. He looks at you.

Before you can answer Gary the head usher says: 'I just want to be honest, I thought the tech had some moments today.' Gary says this with the gentle sincerity of a man who believes he is being helpful. You have had a day that included a subwoofer, a meme about your pastor on a 12-foot screen, Caleb and the batteries, a DC Talk clipboard guy, and BlippiCoin. You nod slowly at Gary. Inside you are somewhere else entirely.

## A

The room is quiet by item three. The campus pastor stops writing by item five. Greg, if present, closes his notebook. When you finish the campus pastor says: 'What do you need from me?' You have a list. You have always had a list. You hand him the list.

## B

Clean. Confident. The room sees someone who runs something. Gary nods along. Gary's nodding has a quality that suggests he is taking credit for something. You do not look at Gary.

## C

Two items. Both significant. Both documented. Both with solutions attached. The campus pastor nods. Greg nods. Gary opens his mouth. The campus pastor raises his hand slightly in Gary's direction. Gary closes his mouth. You file this away.

## D:win

Gary stops. Gary thinks about this. Gary was at the back of the room during second service managing the doors. The stage is at the front of the room. The room has gone quiet in the specific way rooms go quiet when someone has said something that cannot be unsaid. Gary opens his mouth. Gary closes his mouth. After the meeting the campus pastor finds you. 'You didn't have to do that,' he says. 'I know,' you say. 'It was still accurate,' he says. He walks away.

## D:lose

Gary recovers. Gary says: 'I think we all have areas we can improve.' The room nods. You nod. The campus pastor nods. Gary has turned a direct hit into a life lesson for everyone and you are watching it happen in real time.

Around the room: Worship leader reports second service had something. Kids ministry describes the bathroom situation in eleven words. The room receives these eleven words in silence. First Impressions says the door felt warm but the lobby felt cold. Nobody claims the lobby. Someone says the haze was a lot today. Nobody claims the haze.

'Same time next week.'

Four seconds.

Gary moves first.

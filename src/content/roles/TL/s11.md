Services are done. You are running a post-service diagnostic. Your husband appears. He drove separately and came to second service. He is holding two coffees. He hands you one. He doesn't say anything about the lighting or the stream or the meme or Caleb or the battery or the subwoofer or the kid who got knocked over by a sound wave. He just stands next to you while you finish the diagnostic. You drink the coffee. It is the best coffee you have ever had. It is the same coffee you have every day. Context is everything.

Then a man approaches. He is 55 years old. He is large. He has wispy red hair that is doing its best. He introduces himself and tells you he wants to join your tech team. He says he doesn't need training. He says he used to tour with DC Talk. You ask in what capacity. He is vague about the capacity. What is clear is that he toured with DC Talk in some capacity, he has been in rooms with professional equipment, and he would like to start next Sunday.

## A

He takes it. He does not look thrilled about it. He shadows the following Sunday with the energy of a man completing a formality he finds unnecessary. By the end of service two you are genuinely unsure who is learning from whom.

## B

He sits down next Sunday with the confidence of a man who has been in rooms like this before. There is one moment during the second song where he does something to the mix that perhaps made sense in 1995 on a tour bus outside of Tulsa. The congregation tilts their heads slightly. He puts his hand up before you can reach the board. 'I got it,' he says. He does get it. Eventually.

## C

His LinkedIn lists his DC Talk role as 'Production Associate — Consultant.' This tells you nothing. His Facebook is public. His Facebook is extensive. He was definitely around DC Talk. He is in several photos. In most of them he is slightly out of frame. You find one where he is fully in frame. He is holding a clipboard. You stare at the clipboard photo for longer than you should. You text him Monday: 'Welcome to the team.'

## D:win

You are in Cancun. You receive three texts during your first Sunday off in two years. The first is from DeShawn: 'it's going ok.' The second is from Kayla: 'he's interesting.' The third is from the campus pastor: 'who is the large man in the booth.' You put your phone face down on a beach chair. You pick up a drink with an umbrella in it. You have made peace with whatever is happening in that booth. For today. Just today.

## D:lose

You are in Cancun. You receive seven texts, two missed calls, and a voice memo from Marcus that is 3 minutes and 47 seconds long. You listen to the voice memo. The voice memo describes, in significant technical detail, something that happened during second service that Marcus believes you need to know about immediately. You are standing on a beach in Cancun. You know about it immediately. You finish your drink. You book an earlier flight home.

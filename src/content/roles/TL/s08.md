Seventeen minutes between services. You have a checklist. The checklist has 14 items. You realistically have time for 10.

You are on item 3 when you notice him. A kid. Maybe six years old. On the stage. Not near the stage. On the stage. He has found the worship leader's mic stand and is using it as a dance partner. He is now moving toward the keyboard. You scan the lobby. Not a single adult is tracking this child. The kid has found the keyboard. The kid has found that the keyboard makes sounds. The kid is making sounds.

You have 16 minutes left of turnover. The keyboard is on. The stage is live. This child is unsupervised and curious and getting closer to things that cost more than his college fund.

## A

You leave the booth. You cross the room. You arrive at the stage and make eye contact with the kid. The kid looks at you. The kid runs. Not away from the stage. Deeper onto the stage. You are now chasing a six-year-old around the drum kit during turnover. DeShawn is watching from the booth. DeShawn will tell this story.

## B

The announcement echoes through the building. One of the parents is the kid's mom. She is mortified. The kid does not look mortified. The kid waves at the room. Several people wave back. You have spent venue mic time on a six-year-old.

## C

The keyboard goes silent mid-note. The kid looks at the keyboard. The kid looks at his hands. The kid does not understand what happened. The kid presses the keys again. Nothing. The kid accepts this and moves on to the drum kit. You have solved one problem and created a different problem.

## D:win

You reach into your tech bag and produce a 9-volt battery. You tell the kid you're going to teach him the most important skill in all of audio production. The kid does this without hesitation. The kid's expression in the half second after contact is one of the most pure and complete experiences of surprise you have ever witnessed on a human face. He pulls back. He looks at the battery. He looks at you. 'It works,' you say. The kid spends the rest of second service testing every 9-volt battery in your tech bag with complete focus and dedication. He has contributed meaningfully to your inventory management. You will never tell his father how.

## D:lose

The kid tells his father immediately after service with the enthusiasm of someone sharing the best thing that has ever happened to them. He is pointing at you from across the lobby. His father follows the finger. The pastor looks at the kid. The pastor looks at you. You make eye contact with the pastor. The pastor's expression moves through several distinct phases. He mouths something in your direction. You are not certain what he mouthed but you are fairly certain it was not a compliment.

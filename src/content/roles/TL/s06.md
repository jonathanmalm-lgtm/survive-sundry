Third song. The room is engaged. Everything is going suspiciously smooth.

Then it happens. The ProPresenter slide advances and instead of the verse lyrics the screen behind the worship leader displays a meme. Not just any meme. It was the single greatest meme you've ever made — a meme about your pastor. You shared it in a group chat to two coworkers who also thought it was hilarious. It was also incredibly disrespectful. And your pastor is now seeing it on a 12-foot screen.

The slide gets fixed. The song continues. But the damage has been done. The pastor walks slowly toward the booth. He arrives at the booth door. He looks at you. He says: 'What was that?'

## A

He listens. He does not interrupt. When you finish he is quiet for a moment. 'Who else is in the group chat?' he asks. You tell him. He nods. He walks away. He stops after three steps. He turns around. 'It was funny,' he says. 'Don't do that again.' He walks away. You do not fully trust the compliment. The compliment is real. You still do not fully trust it.

## B

He looks at you. He looks at the screen where the lyrics are now correctly displayed. He looks back at you. 'A file,' he says. 'Yes,' you say. He nods slowly in the way of a man who has decided to accept an answer he does not fully believe. 'Clean it up,' he says. He walks away.

## C

This is not a lie. DeShawn was loading files this morning. Something did get in there. You are not technically saying DeShawn made the graphic. You are allowing the pastor to arrive at that conclusion on his own. DeShawn hears you say this from across the room. DeShawn looks at you. You look at DeShawn. You look away first. DeShawn will not bring this up today. DeShawn will bring this up eventually. DeShawn will bring this up at a time and in a place entirely of his choosing.

## D:win

The pastor's expression shifts from confused to concerned. 'I'll talk to him,' he says. He installs a shared equipment policy that actually needed to exist anyway. Something good came from something terrible. You accept this quietly and tell no one. The youth pastor never figures out it was you.

## D:lose

The youth pastor figures out it was you within 48 hours. He texts you Monday afternoon: 'really?' Just that. One word. You type and delete six different responses before settling on: 'Can we get coffee?' He responds: 'sure.' The coffee happens Wednesday. It is the most uncomfortable 45 minutes of your month. He is not angry. He is disappointed in the specific way that is so much worse than angry.

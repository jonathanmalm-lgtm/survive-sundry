Turnover between services. You are checking the campus pastor's calendar as part of your standard weekly review — something you do every Sunday because Monday morning is always too late to fix something you noticed Sunday.

You find a problem. Thursday afternoon. The campus pastor has a counseling session scheduled at 2:00 PM with a congregant who has been on the waiting list for six weeks. The campus pastor also has a budget review meeting with the elder board scheduled at 2:00 PM on Thursday. You booked both. Different days. You checked. You don't know how this happened. It happened.

Both parties received confirmation emails. Both parties have the time blocked.

## A

You make two calls during turnover. The counseling appointment moves to the following Monday. The congregant is gracious. You note that she has been waiting six weeks and flag it for the campus pastor with a note about the extended wait. He follows up personally. This is the right call, handled correctly.

## B

The campus pastor reads your text between services. He responds during turnover: 'Move the counseling to Monday. I'll reach out personally.' He does reach out personally. The congregant is grateful. You have protected his relationship with her by making it his call rather than yours.

## C

Monday morning, 9:02 AM. The counseling appointment arrives. The budget meeting has also arrived. The campus pastor's office has two things happening in it simultaneously, which is not physically possible. The campus pastor calls you from his office door. His tone is not angry. His tone is specifically, carefully not angry. That specific careful not-angry is worse.

## D:win

The calendar block creates a 3-hour open window that the campus pastor did not know he had. He uses two hours of it to prepare for Sunday's message, which he has been behind on all week. After service he finds you: 'Who blocked Thursday?' 'Scheduling maintenance,' you say. He looks at you for a moment. 'Okay,' he says. He means more than that.

## D:lose

The elder board calls Thursday morning to confirm the 2:00 PM meeting. The campus pastor confirms. At 1:45 PM the campus pastor looks at his calendar and sees the block. He calls you. 'Did you block my Thursday?' 'Scheduling maintenance,' you say. There is a silence. 'The elders are here,' he says. 'I know,' you say. 'This is a longer conversation,' he says. The budget meeting happens. The longer conversation also happens.

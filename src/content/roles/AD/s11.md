Services are done. You are closing out the morning — updating records, closing work orders, noting what needs to follow-up — when the youth pastor appears at your office door.

He wants to know if he can use the main auditorium for youth group on Wednesday evenings starting in three weeks. He says his current room 'doesn't have the right energy.' He says the worship team would benefit from 'more stage time.' He says he only needs it until around 9:00 PM.

The main auditorium is booked Tuesday evenings for women's Bible study setup. Thursday evenings for marriage group and young adults. Wednesday evenings the cleaning crew comes through at 7:30 PM. The cleaning crew has a contract. The contract specifies access. The youth pastor is looking at you with the optimism of someone who has not yet been told about the cleaning crew contract.

## A

He receives the no with better grace than you expected. He follows up with: 'Is there a timeline for when it might be available?' You tell him you'll look at the schedule and get back to him Thursday. You get back to him Thursday. You found a window. It's the second Wednesday of each month, 6:30 to 8:00 PM. He takes it. He sends a thank-you email that is genuinely warm. You read it twice.

## B

He explains the vision. He is sincere. The vision is good. You explain the constraints. The constraints are real. Together you find the second-Wednesday window. He is pleased. He thanks you. When he leaves your office you sit for a moment and think about the fact that the hardest part of this job and the best part of this job are sometimes the same thing.

## C

He submits through the process. The process takes four days. On day three he emails you directly to check on the status. The process has not moved because the process requires your action and you have not taken the action. You take the action on day four. He gets a partial answer on day five. The answer is the same answer you would have given on Sunday. He is a little less warm about it by day five.

## D:win

You say yes. He is delighted. He leaves. Monday morning you look at the schedule and realize the second and fourth Wednesdays are the only windows and they're both currently blocked. You call him Monday. You apologize. You give him the actual window — second Wednesdays only, 6:30 to 8:00. He is gracious. He says: 'Thank you for figuring it out.' You accept this graciously. You do not explain the original yes.

## D:lose

You say yes. He emails twelve parents Monday morning announcing Wednesday nights in the main auditorium. Parents send seventeen responses by noon. Tuesday morning the women's Bible study setup crew calls to ask about the new youth group equipment left in the corner of the auditorium. You call the youth pastor. The campus pastor calls you. You are explaining the yes while untangling the implications of the yes. The youth pastor is gracious. He is confused but gracious. He is the only one who is gracious.

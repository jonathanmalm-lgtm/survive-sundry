Second service is running. You are at your desk. You have twelve items on your list and you are on item three when Chad arrives at your office door.

Chad is a bartender at Chili's but he's very into finance. Chad has been pitching staff all morning. Chad found you because your door was open and you are the administrator and Chad has done some research and believes the administrator 'controls the budget' and he'd like to talk about BlippiCoin.

Chad has a deck. The deck is about BlippiCoin — a cryptocurrency backed by Blippi, the children's entertainer. Chad says 'faith-based finance' twice. Chad describes himself as working in 'finance and also some tech stuff.'

You have a policy on solicitation in church facilities. You wrote it. You know exactly where it is.

## A

You print the policy from your laptop. You hand it to Chad. Chad reads it. Chad looks at you. 'Is this real?' he asks. 'Yes,' you say. Chad leaves. He leaves the deck behind. You put the deck in your records as evidence of policy application. This is perhaps the most administrator thing that has ever happened.

## B

5 minutes becomes 11. Chad is thorough. His deck has a slide titled 'FAITH-BASED FINANCE: WHY NOW.' You ask one clarifying question. You should not have asked a clarifying question. The clarifying question produces four slides you hadn't seen yet.

## C

Chad accepts this. Chad emails you the deck at 11:52 PM. The subject line is 'FOR YOUR REVIEW — URGENT OPPORTUNITY.' You respond Monday with one sentence referencing the solicitation policy. Chad follows up once more. Then stops. You file the exchange.

## D:win

You have invested $200 in a cryptocurrency backed by a man in an orange and blue outfit who teaches children about excavators. You did this from your desk in the church office. You did this using the same device on which you track the church's operational finances. BlippiCoin is worth $124,000 by the month-later meeting. You tell no one for two weeks. Then you file a tithe on the $124,000 because that seems correct. The accounting on this is genuinely unusual.

## D:lose

You have invested $200 in a cryptocurrency backed by a man in an orange and blue outfit who teaches children about excavators. BlippiCoin is worth $0. You are the administrator. You manage church finances. You invested in BlippiCoin. You tell no one. You will carry this specific irony for a significant amount of time. Chad follows up twice. You do not respond to Chad. Chad is still a bartender at Chili's.

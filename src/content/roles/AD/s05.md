Eight minutes before first service. You are doing a final pass on the week's logistics when you notice it.

Two events are booked in the same room for the same Thursday evening. The marriage group. And the young adults ministry. Both have confirmed. Both have sent reminder emails. Both have chairs set up in their respective configurations in — and this is the problem — the same room, which physically cannot be configured in two different ways simultaneously, and which will discover this problem at 6:55 PM on Thursday when both leaders arrive with their groups.

This was not your booking. This was here before you. But you saw it. And you are the administrator. That's how it works.

## A

Young adults moves to the overflow room. Marriage group stays. Both leaders receive the news with varying degrees of grace. One of them says 'no problem.' One of them says 'we've used that room for three years.' You say: 'I know. I'm sorry. Thursday at 6:55 was going to be worse.' There is a pause. 'Fair,' they say.

## B

Both parties respond within twenty minutes. Young adults moves. Marriage group stays. Everyone is fine. You have resolved a conflict that didn't happen yet. You add a calendar audit to your monthly checklist. You find two more conflicts. You resolve them before they become conflicts. Nobody knows. This is the job.

## C

Thursday at 6:55 PM. Both leaders arrive. Both have people. Both have chairs. They call the campus pastor. The campus pastor calls you. You are watching a movie. The movie can wait. The conversation that follows cannot. You resolve it in forty minutes over the phone. The campus pastor thanks you. The thanks is warm. The timing is 8:12 PM on a Thursday.

## D:win

The campus pastor responds: 'Good catch. Can you resolve it?' 'Already on it,' you reply. He responds: 'Good.' That's the whole exchange. You file this as a win. The campus pastor's 'good' has weight. You have learned to read the weight.

## D:lose

The campus pastor responds to both parties before you can act. He resolves it himself — quickly, smoothly — and then replies to your email. His reply is three words: 'Handled it. Thanks.' The 'thanks' is warm. The 'handled it' is a note. You know the note. You have received the note before. You add a calendar audit to your Monday checklist.

Service is running. You are in the lobby doing what you do during service — managing logistics, watching the lobby, being available for the problems that didn't fit anywhere else.

Ron finds you. Eleven years at Execution Church. Ron has a facilities request. The thermometer in Row 4, Section B has been reading cold for three weeks. Ron has logged it twice through the app. Neither log has received a response. Ron is not here to complain — Ron wants it resolved. Ron says this church runs very well operationally for the most part, and the thermostat situation suggests a gap in the facilities response system that, as the administrator, you should know about.

Ron tells you what he tithes each month. It is your entire salary. From one person. Ron would like you to know that.

## A

You handle it Monday morning. The facilities team receives your direct request at 8:02 AM. The work order is created by 9:15 AM. The thermostat is serviced Tuesday afternoon. Ron receives no communication about this. On the following Sunday, Row 4 Section B is the appropriate temperature. Ron nods at it in a way that suggests he noticed. He does not say anything. He does not have to.

## B

Ron walks through the app with you in the lobby during the sermon. It takes six minutes. Ron successfully submits a new request. The request is routed correctly. Ron thanks you. 'I should have come to you earlier,' Ron says. 'The app is there if you need it,' you say. 'I'll remember that,' Ron says. Ron will remember that. Ron will tell other people that. This is worth more than six minutes.

## C

Ron receives the facilities email address. Ron emails facilities directly. Facilities responds in eleven days. The thermostat is addressed two weeks later. Ron sends you a follow-up on week three. You have forgotten this conversation entirely. Ron has not.

## D:win

The campus pastor receives the email. He responds: 'Thanks — but the facilities process is fine, just needs a follow-up.' He handles the thermostat himself, which is not the intended outcome but is a functional outcome. He does not mention the CC to the elders. He does mention it the following week in a staff meeting, briefly, with a tone that makes clear this was an edge case rather than a pattern. Ron's thermostat is fixed.

## D:lose

Three elders respond to your email. One of them has questions about the facilities response SLA. One of them wants to be added to the facilities request distribution list. One of them calls the campus pastor. The campus pastor calls you during the sermon. You step outside. The thermostat issue has become a governance conversation. Ron's thermostat is eventually fixed. It takes three weeks. Ron submits a facilities request through the app. Nobody monitors the app.

Second service is running. You have twenty minutes and a habit.

Your Sunday facility walk happens during second service. Every Sunday. You walk the building — lobby, hallways, restrooms, classrooms, stage loading dock, exterior. You have a mental checklist. The checklist has never been written down because writing it down would make it a policy and then it would require training and sign-off and a committee review. As a mental checklist it is just something you do.

Today you notice: the men's restroom second stall has an 'Out of Order' sign that was not there this morning. The sign is handwritten. Nobody told you about this. The side exit near the kids wing has a propped-open door. It has been propped open with a rock. The rock looks like it was placed there intentionally. You do not know who placed it. You also do not know what the lobby looked like an hour ago and what it looks like now and whether those two states match.

## A

You find: the restroom is an actual plumbing issue, reported to facilities before you reach the lobby. The propped door has a note behind it — the kids ministry leader left it for a delivery. The delivery didn't come. You close the door. You note the rock in your facility log. The rock is now in the facility log. The facility log is the most complete record of unusual objects on this property.

## B

You address the restroom — it's a plumbing issue, you get a work order in. The propped door you close without investigating further. Monday you'll find out about the delivery. You file 'propped door, side exit, unknown' in your notes. Your notes are extensive. Your notes are also the only place this lives.

## C

Monday morning. The restroom second stall is still out of order. Six people used the broken stall over the weekend, producing two complaints and one very wet situation that Facilities is now managing. The propped door was open during second service turnover. You find out about the door from Dave, who was in the parking lot and noticed a child walk out unescorted. The child was fine. Dave followed up. Dave always follows up. Dave did not know who to call. Dave called you. It is Monday morning and Dave is calling you and the campus pastor is also calling you.

## D:win

Your Gary itemization is specific and documented. The conversation after the H&M meeting takes four minutes. You are direct and non-accusatory. Gary listens. Gary is quiet for a moment. Gary says: 'I was trying to help.' 'I know,' you say. 'Can we agree on a protocol for volunteer-initiated physical changes?' Gary says: 'I'll write it up.' Gary writes it up Tuesday. It is more detailed than anything you would have produced.

## D:lose

Gary receives the itemization with the energy of a man who has been preparing his defense since 8:00 AM. Gary has a counter-list. Gary's counter-list has seventeen items. Gary's counter-list includes the thermostat in Row 4 Section B, which Gary filed a facilities request about in 2023 and which was not addressed for six months. You were not the administrator in 2023. Gary knows you were not the administrator in 2023. Gary included it anyway.

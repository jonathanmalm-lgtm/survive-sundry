Seven-thirty means everyone was supposed to be here at seven-fifteen. You know this. They know this. The volunteers who arrive at 7:31 will apologize with the specific energy of people who are not sorry. That's fine. You account for it.

What you did not account for: three people are not here and are not going to be here. Janet texted at 6:45 to say she has "a thing." Kevin has not texted. Kevin is never going to text. Sandra texted at 7:12 to say she "forgot it was her Sunday," which is a sentence that should not be possible given that she confirmed on Thursday. You have a screenshot. You will not use the screenshot.

That's a greeter, a kid-check-in volunteer, and the person who runs the connection card table. These are not decorative positions. You have twelve other volunteers standing in front of you. They are smiling. They are caffeinated. They did not sign up for extra work today.

## A
You pull up your clipboard and redistribute without preamble. "Marcus, you're on door three today. Linda, kid check-in. Tom, connection cards — I'll walk you through it, takes two minutes." Everyone adjusts. Nobody argues. Tom says "I've actually done it before" with quiet pride. You make a note to put Tom on the official schedule. This is how the volunteer pipeline has always worked: emergency, competence, promotion.

## B
You decide the gaps will fill themselves organically because people are good and the Holy Spirit provides. The door to the east entrance stands open and ungreeted for forty minutes. Eight people walk in without being acknowledged. One of them is a first-time guest who will later describe the experience as "like going to a haunted house that had a band." The connection card table has cards on it but no pens. These facts are related.

## C
You smile at the group and say, gently, that you're a little short-staffed this morning and you'd love it if anyone felt called to stretch their role a little. Three people immediately volunteer. One of them is Brenda, who was already doing two jobs and says "I love doing three things" in a way that makes you slightly concerned about Brenda. You say thank you and mean it. You make a mental note to check on Brenda at turnover.

## D:win
You text the pastor: "We need three bodies — door 3, kids check-in, connection cards. Can you do a quick 'who wants to serve today' moment before you greet?" He texts back "love it." He does it from the stage at 9:52 with the specific warm authority that only senior pastors have. Seven hands go up. You have more volunteers than positions. Gary, already serving as head usher, raises his hand too. You do not acknowledge Gary's hand. Gary lowers it without incident.

## D:lose
The pastor misunderstands the text and announces from the stage that the church is "desperately short on volunteers across all areas" and directs people to the connection card table, which is unstaffed. Fourteen people go to the connection card table. Nobody is there. They stand in a loose, confused cluster for four minutes until you sprint across the lobby to help them. You are smiling the entire time. This is the job.

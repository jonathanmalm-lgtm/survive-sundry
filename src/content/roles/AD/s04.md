The team huddle is at 9:00 AM. You are here. You have been here since 7:15 because you are always here since 7:15.

The tech leader steps forward and acknowledges staffing gaps. 'God won't give you more than you can handle.' 1 Corinthians 10:13. That verse is about resisting temptation. You have a note about it in a folder somewhere. This is not the time.

The campus pastor produces a $5 Chick-fil-A gift card. Staff meeting consensus. He calls Sandra's name. Sandra is the volunteer coordinator for the setup team who arrived at 6:00 AM and has personally unfolded forty-seven chairs without being asked. She receives the card with the expression of someone who didn't know they were being watched but is very glad they were.

Then Greg is introduced. Former worship pastor at a well-known church that recently imploded. He is here to 'observe and encourage.' He is wearing a very good jacket. You are already thinking about the fact that Greg is in the building today and Greg is not in your system. Greg has no background check on file. Greg has no volunteer release form. Greg has no emergency contact information. Greg is in your building.

## A

Greg is slightly surprised. He is not offended. He gives you his information with the efficiency of a man who has been in enough church systems to understand the logic. 'You're thorough,' he says. 'Yes,' you say. The campus pastor finds out Monday and says: 'Good call.' He does not thank you. He doesn't have to. This is the job.

## B

Greg observes. Greg is warm. Greg is unofficially in your building without documentation. Nothing happens today. Something happening was never the likely outcome. The unlikely outcome is still possible, which is why you have the form, which is why not using it bothers you for the rest of the day.

## C

The campus pastor receives the flag. He is on the phone at the time. He says 'yep, good point' in the way of someone who has filed something without processing it. Greg leaves the building undocumented. The campus pastor asks about it Monday. You have already created a protocol. You show him the protocol. He nods. He says 'good.' He walks away.

## D:win

Greg looks at the form. Greg is quiet for a moment. Greg fills out the form. Greg's handwriting is very neat. You file it. The campus pastor sees this exchange from across the lobby. He finds you after the huddle. 'Did you just give Greg a background check form?' 'Volunteer protocol,' you say. He looks at you. 'I know,' he says. He sounds like he means something more than he's saying.

## D:lose

Greg looks at the form. The campus pastor sees this exchange from across the lobby. The campus pastor arrives before Greg can respond. 'We don't need to do that today,' the campus pastor says, with the specific warmth of someone extinguishing a fire. Greg smiles. The campus pastor gives you a look that contains an entire private conversation. That conversation happens Monday morning.
